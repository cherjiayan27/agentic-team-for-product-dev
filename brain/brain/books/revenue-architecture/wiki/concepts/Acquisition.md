# Acquisition

## Definition

Revenue generated from **new customers** — i.e. from growing the size of the customer base. One of the [[Three Components of Growth]] in a recurring revenue business.

## Properties

- **Dependency:** on the organisation's ability to *promise* value (value proposition, marketing, sales).
- **Cost profile:** very high — Jacco's baseline assumption is that **acquisition cost = 100% of first-year revenue acquired**. Profit on a newly acquired customer is therefore typically zero in year 1.
- **Growth curve:** in Jacco's BestCo case study, acquisition grows approximately linearly; its share of total growth *declines* as the business matures.
- **Dominance:** dominates Stage 1 of the [[Four Stages of Growth]]. Loses dominance to [[Retention]] around $10M ARR and to [[Expansion]] around $100M ARR.

## Common misuse

When growth slows, leadership often over-invests in acquisition (hiring more salespeople) instead of addressing the underlying shift toward retention being the real engine. This is a direct anti-pattern flagged in [[First Principles of Recurring Revenue]] Principle 2 and the discussion of short-tenure [[Chief Revenue Officer]] incentives.

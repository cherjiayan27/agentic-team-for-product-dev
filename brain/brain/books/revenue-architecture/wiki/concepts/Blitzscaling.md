# Blitzscaling

## Definition

Extreme-pace growth strategy popularised by Reid Hoffman and Chris Yeh (*Blitzscaling*, 2018). Applied when being **first to scale** — not first to market — can produce a dominant, defensible market position. Requires large accessible TAM and winner-takes-all dynamics (network effects, platform lock-in).

## Magnitudes

- Annual growth rate **>1,000%**
- CMGR **>21.2%**
- Higher than [[Hypergrowth]], [[T2D3 Growth Trend]], or anything else in Table 9.2

## Mechanics

- Excessive marketing and sales spend
- Aggressive hiring (quality bar relaxed in the name of speed)
- Intentional short-term unprofitability in exchange for market share
- Network-effect exploitation as the primary moat

## Examples (from Ch 9)

- Spotify
- Uber
- Airbnb
- Shopify
- OpenAI

## When it's justified

- **Winner-takes-all markets** — consumer and professional-user spaces with strong network effects
- **First-to-scale wins** — not all markets reward this; in enterprise B2B selling, first-to-scale often doesn't beat first-to-execute-well

## Risk

Financial collapse. If the market doesn't tip to winner-takes-all, the investment doesn't produce the intended moat and the company has burned capital for no defensive advantage. Many blitzscale attempts fail quietly.

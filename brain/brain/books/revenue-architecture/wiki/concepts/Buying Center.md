# Buying Center

## Definition

The group of individuals within a customer organisation who participate in a purchase, renewal, or expansion decision. In B2B, research shows this can include up to 20 people. A methodology that operates independently of job titles and focuses instead on **roles played by stakeholders**.

## The nine roles (Table 8.2)

Each role maps to specific [[SPICED]] elements (S=Situation, P=Pain, I=Impact, CE=Critical Event, D=Decision):

| Role | SPICED focus | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **Initiator** | S, P | First to suggest or initiate contact |
| **User** | S, P, I | Will directly use the product/service |
| **[[Champion]]** | S, P, I | Internal advocate helping the seller |
| **[[Decider]]** | I, CE | Makes the final purchasing decision |
| **Gatekeeper** | I, CE | Controls or vetoes the decision process |
| **Influencer** | I, CE | Provides information or evaluation criteria |
| **Executive Buyer** | I, CE, D | Owns realising the Impact in the business |
| **Approver** | I, D | Authorises actions of deciders/buyers |
| **Purchaser** | I, D | Handles the actual procurement process |

Roles can overlap — one person can hold multiple. In smaller companies an Initiator might also be Champion, Decider, and Purchaser.

## Why it matters

- **Title-blind.** A Director in one company is an Initiator; in another, a Decider. Focusing on the role, not the title, prevents misreading the opportunity.
- **Maps to SPICED.** The Buying Center is literally how [[SPICED]]'s D (Decision) element unpacks in a multi-stakeholder deal.
- **Sequencing drives velocity.** Knowing who comes into the process when — and why — enables proactive outreach (Figure 8.19).

## Decision-making process (Figure 8.19)

Typical mid-touch sequence:

1. **Initiator** discovers the product at an event — owns S, P
2. **Champion** evaluates via demo/discovery — adds I
3. **Decider** enters, provoked by Champion — adds CE
4. **Executive Buyer / Approver** enters at the end — owns D

The seller's job at each handoff: equip the internal person to sell to the next stakeholder (see [[Rational vs Emotional Impact]]'s curiosity-gap technique).

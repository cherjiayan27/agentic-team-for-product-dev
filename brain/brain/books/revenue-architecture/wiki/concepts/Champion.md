# Champion

## Definition

The individual within a customer account who internally advocates for the product. Distinct from the **Benefactor** (the person or team that derives impact from the product). The Champion may or may not be the Benefactor; often they're different people.

## Why Champions matter

- **Acquisition depends on them.** Enterprise sales move forward because a Champion internally sells the purchase, manages stakeholders, and signals intent.
- **Renewals depend on them.** Without an internal voice, a renewal has no internal advocate when budget cuts arrive.
- **Cross-sell and Resell both hinge on the Champion.** Introducing the product to a new team is a Champion referral. A new Champion may replace an existing one and require fresh buy-in.

## Champion churn is a major risk

When a Champion leaves their role (promotion, new company, role change), the account is structurally vulnerable:

- **Threat:** the new person in the role may be tasked with cutting OPEX and has no emotional connection to your product. Fast path to churn.
- **Opportunity:** the former Champion, now at a new company, becomes a high-quality acquisition target — often a warm [[Acquisition|new-logo]] win. This is also why Resell (one of the [[Four Types of Expansion]]) matters.

## Operational implication

- **Track Champion status at account level.** Know when they change roles.
- **Act fast on Champion changes.** Reselling the new Champion is time-sensitive.
- **Build multi-threaded relationships.** Depending on a single Champion is fragile. CSMs and AMs should cultivate 2–3 internal relationships per account.

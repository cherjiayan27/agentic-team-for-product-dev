# Critical Event

## Definition

A specific deadline or milestone creating urgency in the customer's decision process. The **CE** in [[SPICED]]. Distinct from a Compelling Event — the difference drives forecast accuracy.

## Critical vs Compelling vs Mandatory

Four priority levels (§8.2.3):

| Level | Name | What it does | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| 4 | **Business as Usual** | No urgency; product is "nice-to-have" | Routine operations |
| 3 | **Compelling Event** | Opportunity; no clear deadline | Upgrade to new tech |
| 2 | **Critical Event** | Urgency from risk/loss | Security breach; contract expiry |
| 1 | **Mandatory Event** | Severe consequence of non-action | Regulatory compliance deadline |

## The carrot-vs-stick distinction

- **Compelling** = carrot (pulls forward by opportunity)
- **Critical** = stick (pushes forward by urgency to avoid negative outcome)
- **Mandatory** = stick with severe penalty

Compelling events don't close deals predictably. Critical events do.

## How to test

Two-question test:
1. "When do you need this by?"
2. "What happens if you miss that date?"

If the customer hesitates on #2, it's Compelling (not Critical). If there's a clear consequence, it's Critical.

## Priority = f(Impact, Time)

```
Priority = Impact × Time
```

When Priority crosses a threshold, a Compelling event becomes Critical (Figure 8.17). Unlike budget and ROI (which stay relatively fixed), priority **fluctuates** in SaaS — which is why GTM teams used to ownership-era selling misforecast.

## Why teams get this wrong

- They don't probe when a date is mentioned ("you said you need this by Aug 22 — what's happening then?")
- They confuse renewal dates with Critical Events (usually Compelling — service doesn't cut off instantly)
- What's Critical for the Decider (getting fired) can be merely Compelling for the stakeholder team

## Dynamic priority implication

"Going dark" after a proposal doesn't mean the deal is lost. It means the customer's priority dropped below threshold. Something will eventually re-raise it (e.g. a security breach, a missed deadline elsewhere). GTM teams should reassess rather than write off.

# Customer Success Motions

## Definition

How a CS organisation delivers ongoing value to retain customers and enable expansion. Organised along two channels (community / helpdesk) and three approaches (volume / vertical / account-based). The post-sale half of a [[5 Touch GTM Motions|GTM motion]].

## Two channels

- **Community** — catch-all for self-service support. Knowledge base at the centre, often with chatbot front-end. Power users, experts, team members converge to solve problems together. As users help each other, support load drops.
- **Helpdesk** — the company's "emergency room." Staffed on-call. Ticketing systems and live chat. Official support channel.

## Three approaches

- **Volume-Based** — one CSM per up-to-500 accounts. Efficiency is absolute. Community is the front line; helpdesk is secondary. Fits [[Low Touch GTM Motion]].
- **Vertical-Based** — CSM specialises in one industry (healthcare, financial services, manufacturing). Deep domain knowledge. Community and helpdesk integrated for industry-specific needs. Fits [[Medium Touch GTM Motion]] / [[High Touch GTM Motion]].
- **Account-Based** — CSM dedicated to one F500 account (Disney, Amazon). Understands org chart and business objectives. Community as advocacy platform; helpdesk escalates to the dedicated CSM. Fits [[Dedicated Touch GTM Motion|Dedicated Touch]].

## Ideal: mix of all three

Most mature GTM motions blend volume-based CS for smaller accounts with account-based for largest. Vertical-based appears when industry specialisation creates enough complexity to require it.

## CS covers many activities

- Onboarding
- Adoption and engagement
- Customer retention
- Expansion and upsell
- Customer advocacy programs
- Feedback and insights gathering
- Health scoring and risk mitigation
- Customer education
- Account management
- Personalised customer experiences

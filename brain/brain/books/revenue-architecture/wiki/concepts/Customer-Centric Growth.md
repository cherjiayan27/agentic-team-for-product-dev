# Customer-Centric Growth

## Definition

A growth orientation in which revenue is treated as an **organic outcome** of delivering impact to customers, rather than as the ultimate objective pursued directly. Required by the recurring revenue model; a nice-to-have in the perpetual model.

## Why it's necessitated (not just virtuous)

In a [[Recurring Revenue]] business the [[Risk Shift]] means the seller must keep delivering value every period to keep getting paid. That makes [[Recurring Impact]] the actual revenue engine — and impact only exists if the customer's outcome is taken seriously. A vendor-centric operation ("close the deal, move on") structurally undermines retention and expansion.

## What it implies operationally

- [[Acquisition]] qualifies for customers who can actually achieve the promised impact (not just who can sign).
- Onboarding, success, product, and engineering are treated as revenue functions because they drive [[Retention]] and [[Expansion]].
- Short-term acquisition pushes that damage long-term customer success (e.g. over-promising at sale, reassigning sales to renewals — see [[Retention]] risk section) are rejected even when they would hit quarterly targets.

# Dedicated Touch GTM Motion

## Definition

The highest-touch motion in the [[5 Touch GTM Motions]] framework. Assembles a **specialised team solely dedicated to a single client** — often incorporating roles from product marketing to engineering to field sales. Used for multi-division F500 accounts where a single engagement model can't cover the complexity.

## Canonical example

Disney — a customer that includes ESPN, Disney Parks, ABC Television, Hulu, and more as separate divisions. Each division is effectively its own customer, but coordination value exists at the parent level. A Dedicated Touch team covers the full account including its cross-divisional dynamics.

## Engine

**Relationships.** What people talk about: responsiveness. How it spreads: CxO-to-CxO referral.

## Unit economics

- **ACV:** >$1M (typically multiple $M per division)
- **Team cost:** $600K–$2M annual team cost for named-account teams, before dedicated engineering/product support
- **Deal volume:** 1s — literally one or a handful of accounts per team
- **Cost to serve:** $100K+ per customer per year (Figure 10.10)

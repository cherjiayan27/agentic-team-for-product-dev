# Expansion

## Definition

Additional revenue from **existing customers beyond their renewal baseline** — upgrades, cross-sell, seat growth, usage-based scaling. One of the [[Three Components of Growth]] in a recurring revenue business.

## Properties

- **Dependency:** on the organisation's ability to *deliver* value continuously and to offer upside beyond the initial contract — see [[Recurring Impact]].
- **Cost profile:** approximately **20% of expanded revenue** in Jacco's baseline assumption. Higher than [[Retention]] cost (~10%) but much lower than [[Acquisition]] cost (~100%).
- **Growth curve:** hyperbolic / polynomial. In the BestCo case it reached $10.8M by 2022, about 40% of the acquisition-driven growth ($24.5M) — a familiar exponential pattern when analysed in isolation.
- **Dominance:** defines Stage 3 of the [[Four Stages of Growth]]. Typically surpasses [[Acquisition]] around **$100M ARR**, about 8 years after the first $1M ARR.

## Why it matters

- At scale, expansion is the largest lever on growth, not acquisition. Jacco's example: Adobe, whose addressable new-customer market is saturated for several products, so growth comes almost entirely from expansion.
- Unlike acquisition, expansion compounds on existing customers — so it multiplies the value of good [[Retention]].

## Risk

As with [[Retention]], reassigning the acquisition (sales) team to handle expansion pushes expansion cost toward acquisition-level cost (~100%), destroying the unit economics. Figure 2.7 shows the profit collapse. Tied to short-tenure [[Chief Revenue Officer]] incentives chasing near-term revenue at the expense of long-term profitability.

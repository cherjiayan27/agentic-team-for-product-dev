# Founder-Led Growth

## Definition

Growth achieved primarily through the founder's personal involvement in operational processes — most commonly sales, but also customer service (Zoom's Eric Yuan answering bugs on social media), marketing, and product. The 2nd [[12 Revenue Breakpoints|revenue breakpoint]].

## Why it works early

- **Trust.** Customers feel direct access to the creator.
- **Responsiveness.** No layers between decision and action.
- **Lead-gen multiplier.** Founder's network + reputation opens doors that paid campaigns can't.

## Why it breaks at scale

- **Over-reliance** on the founder creates a throughput ceiling.
- **Process bypass.** The founder works around the team, producing inconsistencies that accumulate.
- **Counterproductive at Scaleup.** What wins Seed-stage loses at Grownup.

## The transition plan

Reduce founder operational involvement *gradually* without losing the innovative spirit:

- **Customer Advisory Board** — keep customer relationships, share them with the team
- **Team training** — transfer founding stories and rationale to operational staff
- **Speaker circuit** — use the founder for brand and category-defining visibility
- **Thought leadership** — founder voice shapes market perception at scale
- **R&D** — innovation-minded founders lead the next product
- **New markets** — entrepreneurial founders open geographies or segments

## Strategic rule

By the time the company is a Scaleup, **the founder should be actively transferring rather than executing.** Staying too operational past $10M ARR is a common failure mode.

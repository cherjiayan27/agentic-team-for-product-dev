# Four Industrial Revolutions

## Definition

The four generational shifts in factory technology. Jacco uses this taxonomy to argue that today's recurring revenue businesses are transitioning from Factory 3.0 (automation) to Factory 4.0 (cyber-physical, AI/ML-enabled). The [[Recurring Revenue Factory]] concept is a Factory 4.0 idea.

## The four eras (Figure 1.4)

| Era | Name | Technology | Key capability |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1.0** | Mechanisation | Water + steam power | Mechanised labor |
| **2.0** | Electrification | Electric assembly lines | Mass production |
| **3.0** | Automation | Networks, computers, robotics | Production automation |
| **4.0** | Cyber-Physical | Cloud, ML, AI | Intelligent systems |

## Why Golden Era SaaS was Factory 3.0

The Golden Era leaned on **automation** — a Factory 3.0 technology:

- **Email automation** — increase volume of outbound emails
- **Email sequencing** — consistent cadence via templates
- **Marketing automation** — campaign execution at scale
- **Templated outreach** — "quality" via consistency

These enabled a single prospector to do the work of many. But they didn't fundamentally improve *understanding* of the customer — and they drove the [[Means and Methods]] proliferation that broke at scale.

## Factory 4.0 in recurring revenue

Cloud + ML + AI enable:

- **Personalised customer experiences** based on usage patterns
- **Real-time coaching (co-pilots)** for sales and CS
- **Dynamic pricing** responding to demand signals
- **Predictive forecasting**
- **Closed-loop systems** across the [[Bowtie]] (Ch 4 preview + §10.2.6 trends)
- **Automated scenario analysis** over the [[Growth Formula]]

## Strategic implication

Companies still operating as Factory 3.0 (automation-heavy, volume-first) are competing against Factory 4.0 companies (AI-native, intelligence-first). The productivity delta between these generations is the new moat.

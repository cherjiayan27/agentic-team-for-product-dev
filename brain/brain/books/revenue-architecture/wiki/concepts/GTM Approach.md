# GTM Approach

## Definition

The cross-functional integration of marketing, sales, and customer success into one **production-line-style** revenue organisation. Contrasts with the **Departmental Approach**, in which each function operates independently with its own metrics and tools. The GTM Approach is the organisational embodiment of the [[Recurring Revenue Factory]].

## Departmental Approach (what it replaces)

Figure 1.6 shows the traditional model: marketing, sales, and customer success as three separate towers. Each:

- Has its own KPIs
- Has its own tool stack
- Optimises its own objective
- Accumulates its own [[Means and Methods]]

Result: silos, duplicated work, misalignment, customers experiencing disjointed transitions.

**Amplifier:** the 10× headcount growth from Series A → Series D (Figure 1.7, Carta 2021) happens before processes mature. New hires import old methods; silos entrench.

## GTM Approach (what replaces it)

Cross-functional collaboration across the whole [[Bowtie]]:

- **Marketing** generates awareness, qualifies
- **Sales** closes against Impact
- **Customer Success** delivers and expands recurring Impact

Unified around a common objective: **deliver Recurring Impact to attain Recurring Revenue**.

## GTM Motions as production lines

A [[GTM Motion]] is the structured set of activities that span the customer journey within the GTM Approach. Each motion:

- Starts with finding prospects
- Qualifies them
- Closes them
- Delivers Impact
- Renews and expands

Think of GTM motions as production lines in the [[Recurring Revenue Factory]]. Multiple motions can coexist; they must be coordinated via the [[Operating Model]].

## Go fast and go long (§1.3.1)

Different motions are optimised for different velocity profiles — see [[Two Domains of the Bowtie]]:

- Go fast = acquisition velocity (cycle time compression)
- Go long = retention/expansion lifetime (compounding via NRR)

## Why it matters

- **Organisational:** breaks departmental fiefdoms that accumulate around the 10× headcount explosion
- **Financial:** aligns costs across the journey so CAC and CTS are managed holistically
- **Strategic:** anchors the entire org on the common metric — Impact delivery

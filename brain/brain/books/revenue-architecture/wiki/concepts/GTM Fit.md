# GTM Fit

## Definition

The state in which a GTM motion has a repeatable process and a working [[Growth Formula]]. Marks the completion of [[12 Revenue Breakpoints|revenue breakpoints]] 1–6. Sits alongside Product-Market Fit (PMF) as the second canonical fit milestone.

## PMF vs GTMF

- **PMF** — customers want the product enough to pay for it. Achieved typically pre-A-round or early in Startup stage.
- **GTMF** — the company can *reliably* sell and retain customers through a defined motion. Achieved typically around B/C-round at the Startup → Scaleup transition.

## Signals of GTMF achievement

1. **Pricing and packaging locked** — one monetization strategy per $100M ARR tier
2. **Founder operationally decoupled** — team can execute without the founder present
3. **Data Model standardised** — [[Bowtie]] conventions adopted; no more "where did that number come from?" meetings
4. **GTM model chosen** — one motion with ≤3 variants, not 5+ uncoordinated motions
5. **Repeatable process** — consistent results at stable lead volume
6. **Growth Formula per motion** — predictions within reasonable error bars

## Why it matters

- **Investors underwrite GTMF**, not just PMF. Series C typically requires GTMF evidence.
- **Scaling pre-GTMF** burns capital without compounding returns (ACME vs NewCo — see [[Scalability]]).
- **Per-motion.** Each GTM motion must achieve its own GTMF. Launching a new motion resets the clock.

# GTM Maturity Stages

## Definition

Three maturity stages a GTM motion passes through on its way to becoming a revenue factory. Each stage has a primary focus, an ARR/GTM threshold, and a distinct set of KPIs. Maps to Ch 1's revenue factory concept and complements [[12 Revenue Breakpoints]].

## The three stages (Table 10.3)

| Stage | Focus | ARR/GTM motion | Primary KPIs |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1. [[Scalability]]** | Growth via volume + throughput | <$10M | Growth Rate, ARR |
| **2. [[Sustainability]]** | Cost efficiency | <$30M | [[CAC Payback]], [[Net Revenue Retention\|NRR]], [[FCF]] |
| **3. [[Durability]]** | Quality — select customers who achieve recurring impact | >$30M | [[DAU]] usage, [[Gross Revenue Retention\|GRR]] |

## Why stages matter

- **Most Golden-Era companies got stuck at Scalability.** An overflow of venture capital caused leadership to equate ARR and growth rate with maturity — while the underlying motion was still immature.
- **Shifting from Scalability → Sustainability** is what defined the post-2022 SaaS era.
- **Durability is the endgame.** Select for customers who achieve recurring impact. These customers stay, pay more, and refer others. This is how a GTM motion becomes profitable, not just large.

## Stage-appropriate decisions

- **In Scalability**, invest in velocity (more leads, more reps, better conversion)
- **In Sustainability**, invest in cost-per-ARR efficiency (CAC payback <18 months, NRR >100%)
- **In Durability**, invest in impact delivery + ICP refinement (usage depth, GRR >95%)

Investing in the wrong stage wastes capital. Sustainability programs on an unproven motion are premature; scalability programs on a saturating motion compound costs.

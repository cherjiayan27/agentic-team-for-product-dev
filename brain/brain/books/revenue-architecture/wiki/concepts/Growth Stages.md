# Growth Stages

## Definition

The five canonical stages a venture-funded company passes through on its way up the [[S-Curve]]. Each stage is characterised by a specific funding round, revenue band, duration, and expected growth rate. Drives both investment decisions and operational focus.

## The five stages (Table 9.3)

| Stage | Funding | ARR | Length | Growth Rate | Primary goal |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Seed** | Angel | Up to $1M | 1–2 yrs | Achieve $1M | Find out what works (PMF search) |
| **Startup** | A, B | Up to $10M | 2–4 yrs | 100–200% | Find out what works |
| **Scaleup** | C, D, (E) | Up to $300M | 3–6 yrs | 60–100% | Do what works — effectively, efficiently |
| **Grownup** | IPO+ | Beyond $300M | 2–4 yrs | 10–60% | Win the right customers |
| **Enterprise** | Public | Beyond $10B | 20+ yrs | <10% | Expand the market or increase profitability |

## Survival rates (Table 9.4)

From $2M seed:
- **45%** reach PMF (A-round) → Startup
- **27%** reach GTMF (B-round)
- **14%** become a Scaleup (C-round)
- **6%** reach $100M ARR (D-round)
- **1%** goes public (IPO) → Grownup

2021 ratio: 45 A-round companies for every 1 IPO/exit. 2023 ratio: 1:60. Trending worse.

## Operational implications

- Each stage requires different capabilities. A Scaleup leadership team won't run a Grownup well (and vice versa).
- Founders typically transition roles at Scaleup → Grownup (see [[Founder-Led Growth]]).
- [[12 Revenue Breakpoints]] map roughly to stage transitions. Breakpoints 1–6 = PMF → GTMF (Seed → Startup). Breakpoints 7–12 = GTMF → Revenue Factory (Scaleup → Grownup).

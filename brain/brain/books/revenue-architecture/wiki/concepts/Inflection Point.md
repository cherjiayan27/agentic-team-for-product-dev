# Inflection Point

## Definition

The moment on the [[S-Curve]] where growth direction shifts. Typically signals the end of middle-stage exponential compounding and the start of maturity-stage plateau. A pivotal strategic checkpoint — miss it and the company coasts into stagnation; catch it and the company can extend the curve.

## Strategic options at the inflection point

1. **Continue pursuing growth.** Requires new TAM sources:
   - New product launches (Apple: iPhone → App Store → iPad → AirPods)
   - New markets (geographic, vertical)
   - Pricing/packaging changes (Netflix premium, Netflix ads)
   - Acquisitions (Facebook: Instagram, WhatsApp; Uber: Postmates → UberEats expansion)
2. **Pivot to profitability.** Optimise costs, improve margins, return cash to shareholders. Typical for mature Enterprises hitting TAM ceilings.

An **IPO often serves as a transformative inflection event** — new capital enables strategic moves (acquisitions) that extend the S-curve and defer the plateau.

## Signals that an inflection point is near

- Growth rate declining faster than the [[Denominator Effect]] alone explains
- CAC rising without corresponding ARR efficiency gains
- Retention or NRR stabilising at the customer-base saturation point
- Easy-to-reach customers already acquired

# Law of Diminishing Returns

## Definition

In the context of recurring revenue acquisition: **every additional touchpoint — meeting, email, call — beyond a threshold reduces the final win rate**, because each touchpoint carries a conversion rate < 1, and those sub-unit multipliers compound.

## Mechanism

Win rate = product of per-touchpoint conversion rates:

```
Win Rate = CR₁ × CR₂ × ... × CRₙ
```

If average per-meeting CR is 90%, the 12th meeting doesn't add +something — it multiplies the running total by 0.9. The result is *less* than it would have been after 11 meetings.

## Worked example (§7.1.3)

- 20 leads, 90% per-meeting CR, **12 meetings** → **5.6 wins**
- 20 leads, 90% per-meeting CR, **11 meetings** → **6.3 wins** (+12% wins by cutting a meeting)
- 20 leads, 90% per-meeting CR, **13 meetings** → **5.1 wins** (−9% by adding a meeting)

## Consequence

- Adding volume to compensate for weak conversion is self-defeating after a point.
- Reducing meeting count (while holding quality high) can *increase* revenue.
- Attrition from **email sequencing / volume outreach** follows the same pattern — exponential decrease in success rate as action count grows (Figure 7.5).

## The correct response

- **Invest in meeting quality, not meeting count.** Start on time, define objectives up front, close on time, agree on next steps.
- **Get the right people in each meeting** to reduce the need for additional meetings.
- **Reduce automated email blasts.** AI-driven over-contact is a current cause of diminished outbound effectiveness.

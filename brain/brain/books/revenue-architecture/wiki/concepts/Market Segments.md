# Market Segments

## Definition

Identifiable groups of customers sharing common characteristics — industry, company size, geography, buying behaviour, needs. Overlaid on the [[GTM Model]] X-Y framework, market segments guide which [[5 Touch GTM Motions]] will work for which customers.

## Canonical segments (Figure 10.4)

Left-to-right along ACV (lowest to highest):

| Segment | Typical ACV | Common motion |
|---|---|---|
| **Consumers** | $0–$100 | No Touch (self-serve + community) |
| **Pro Users** | $100–$5K | No/Low Touch |
| **Small Teams** | $1K–$15K | Low Touch |
| **VSB** (Very Small Business) | $5K–$15K | Low Touch |
| **SMB** | $15K–$50K | Medium Touch |
| **Mid-Market** | $50K–$150K | Medium/High Touch |
| **Enterprise** | $150K–$500K | High Touch |
| **F500** (Fortune 500) | $500K+ | Dedicated Touch |

## Rules of thumb

- Segments are **not determinative** — an SMB buying a $100K SaaS service needs an Enterprise GTM motion (see §10.1.6). ACV and volume drive motion selection, not segment label.
- **Adjacent segments can be served by adjacent motions.** Moving from SMB to Mid-Market often doesn't require a new motion; moving from Mid-Market to Enterprise typically does.
- **Entering a new segment = new motion = re-navigating [[12 Revenue Breakpoints]].**

## Segmentation on other axes

Beyond size-based segmentation:

- **Vertical / industry** — healthcare, financial services, manufacturing, etc. Drives Vertical-Based [[Customer Success Motions]].
- **Geographic region** — affects sales/marketing laws (some outbound motions restricted by region), timezone coverage needs, in-person presence.
- **Buying behaviour** — technical buyers (developer-led growth) vs business buyers vs procurement-led.

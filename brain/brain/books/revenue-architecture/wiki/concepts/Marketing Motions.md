# Marketing Motions

## Definition

The five canonical B2B marketing approaches, each suited to specific touch-level [[GTM Model|GTM motions]]. Covers how an organisation attracts prospects at different ACV and volume levels.

## The five (§10.1.4)

1. **Inbound Marketing** — SEO, content, paid advertising (SEM + display), webinars, PLG "loops" inside the product. Converts attention into leads via valuable content.
2. **Outbound Marketing** — timed email campaigns to specific customer groups. Lead nurturing, pipeline building, customer retention. Newsletters canonical in B2B.
3. **Targeted Marketing (ABM)** — individual-account customisation; personalised campaigns across known personas at each target account.
4. **Networking** — small informal settings (lunch meetings, industry events, professional societies). Underrated B2B strategy.
5. **Word of Mouth** — customer/influencer advocacy. Organic (brand, CX, influencer partnerships) or amplified (referral programs, affiliate, influencer marketing, TikTok/LinkedIn/X).

## Canonical alignments

From §10.1.4:

- **Inbound → Self-Serve + 1-Stage Inside Sales** (VSB, small teams)
- **Outbound → 2-Stage Outbound Sales** (SMB, vertical SaaS)
- **Targeted (ABM) → Field Sales** (enterprise, with deep ICP understanding — see §8.3.1)
- **Networking + Direct Executive Engagement → Named-Account Sales** (F500, 9–18 month relationship-building timelines)
- **Word of Mouth** — amplifies all motions; is the natural engine for PLG

## Evolving landscape

From §10.2.6 trends:

- **SEO / organic traffic under pressure** — AI content flood, shifting SERP behaviour
- **Inbound saturation** — can no longer sustain growth alone
- **Conversational marketing + AI chatbots** — reshaping Low Touch
- **Outbound saturation** — response rates declining
- **Video content / TikTok** — becoming material for B2B (Adobe, Canva, Grammarly)

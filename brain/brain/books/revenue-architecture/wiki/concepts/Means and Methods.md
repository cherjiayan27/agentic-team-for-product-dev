# Means and Methods

## Definition

The tools (means) and methodologies (methods) that GTM teams use to run their work. Examples: BANT, MEDDIC, CHAMP, SPIN, ABM, ICP, NPS, SEO, SEM, VAR. Each is useful in isolation. The problem is their **proliferation without integration** — the core challenge [[Operating Model]] exists to solve.

## How proliferation happens

Four drivers (§8.1.1):

1. **Hiring imports methods.** Each new person brings practices from previous jobs. Teams across regions/products/segments independently adopt BANT in one area and MEDDIC in another.
2. **Vendors push their own vocabulary.** Every tool promises to change the world; each invents new acronyms for differentiation. 30 tools → 30 vocabularies.
3. **Executives set, then leave.** Fast-growing SaaS averages 18-month executive tenure. By $50M ARR, a company has cycled through multiple CROs/CMOs/CSOs, each leaving their methodology layer.
4. **Consultants benefit from chaos.** Independent consulting firms, new executives, tool vendors, conference speakers — all profit from the complexity, not from resolving it.

## The complexity explosion

Adding a new GTM motion doesn't add to Means/Methods linearly; it **squares the interconnections**:

- One motion (high touch): ABM + ICP + Buying Center + Nurture + MEDDIC + Messaging — manageable
- Two motions (high + low touch): add SEO + Content + Inbound + Nurture + BANT + Freemium → interconnections explode (Figure 8.3)

By $150M ARR most companies run 12+ motions with unmanageable interconnections.

## What to do (and not do)

**Don't:** try to eliminate Means/Methods. They exist because they work in specific contexts.

**Do:** implement an [[Operating Model]] that provides a standardised **Interface** — [[SPICED]] — through which any Means/Method can plug in. BANT reports up as (Budget→D, Need→P, Timeline→CE). MEDDIC reports up as (Metrics→I, Economic Buyer→D, Decision Criteria→I, Decision Process→D, Identify Pain→P, Champion→D). Teams keep their tools; the interface unifies the data.

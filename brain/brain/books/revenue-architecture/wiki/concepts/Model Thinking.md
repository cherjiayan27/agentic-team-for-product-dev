# Model Thinking

## Definition

The discipline of using simplified, combinable representations of reality to understand, explain, and predict complex systems. Drawn from Scott Page's *The Model Thinker*. Applied in Revenue Architecture to the [[Six-Layer Model Stack]].

## Three core principles

1. **Models aren't 100% accurate** — they're simplifications; by construction incomplete
2. **Combining models increases accuracy** — no single model captures a complex system, but overlapping models compensate for each other's gaps
3. **Models are for prediction and scenario-testing** — design, stress-test, iterate before deploying in the real world

## Why models matter for GTM

Before Revenue Architecture, GTM operated on gut feel and trend-following. Model thinking forces:

- **Separation of static and dynamic.** The Revenue Model rarely changes; the GTM Model changes constantly. Operating both under the same decision process is a category error.
- **Scenario analysis.** See [[Scenario Analysis]] — vary one input, see the system-wide output.
- **Cross-model impact.** A change to the Revenue Model propagates up through all layers (see [[Six-Layer Model Stack]] failure propagation).
- **Evidence-based decisions.** First principles + data + math = decisions you can explain and iterate on.

## Engineering analogies

- **Civil engineering** — a suspension bridge = 6 components (piers, anchors, towers, main cable, suspension cables, deck), each with its own mathematical model, combined into a system model
- **Human anatomy** — skeletal + muscular + nervous + cardiovascular models together describe human movement; no single model does

Revenue Architecture applies the same logic: 6 essential models, each a distinct view, combined into one coherent system.

## Warning: model limits

- Models **can't make a bad product great.** They illuminate a path; the product still has to deliver Impact.
- Models **aren't substitutes for judgement.** They inform decisions; humans still make them.
- Models **evolve with data.** A model calibrated on 2012 data may not hold in 2024.

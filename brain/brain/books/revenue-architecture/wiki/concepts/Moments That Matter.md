# Moments That Matter

## Definition

**MTMs.** The small set of customer interactions that disproportionately shape the customer's perception of the product and the provider. Sellers guide buyers through *hundreds* of actions across a customer lifetime, but only **about a dozen actions deeply impact the customer**. Those are the MTMs. Focus-point for the [[Act Small]] leg of the Think-Big-Act-Small-Move-Fast deployment strategy.

## Examples

- **Response time during unexpected downtime** — a slow CS response here can destroy years of goodwill; a fast one creates advocacy
- **First Impact (onboarding)** — the moment the customer experiences the product's value for the first time
- **The diagnose call** — how deeply the seller uncovers Impact (SPICED) shapes the rest of the deal
- **The renewal conversation** — not actually the renewal itself, but the value-conversation before it
- **Champion transition** — how the team handles a Champion leaving (see [[Four Types of Expansion]] Resell)
- **POC / pilot first demo** — the moment the buyer internally presents the solution to stakeholders

## Why MTMs matter

- **Marginal-gain compounding.** Improving a small number of MTMs by 5–10% each produces **disproportionate** customer-experience lift via [[Compound Impact]].
- **Manageable scope.** 10–12 MTMs can actually be blueprinted, measured, and improved. "Improving the customer experience" as a whole cannot.
- **Factory-style process discipline.** Each MTM gets a blueprint (Figure 11.2) — what happens, who's involved, what tools are used, what the success metric is.
- **Cross-functional.** MTMs span marketing / sales / CS boundaries; blueprinting them forces [[GTM Approach]] collaboration.

## MTM blueprint example (Figure 11.2)

A discovery-call blueprint:

1. **Conversation** — opening
2. **Situation questions** (× 2–3)
3. **Pain questions** (× 1–2)
4. **Summarize** → creates a platform
5. **Pitch/Demo** (if customer is ready) OR
6. **Identify Impact** (if customer is in consultative mode) OR
7. **Reset** (if the summary reveals a misalignment)

Marginal improvements at each step compound into better win rate.

## How to choose your MTMs

From Ch 11's practical workflow:

- **Map the customer journey** on the [[Bowtie]]
- **Identify all interactions** between the customer and the company
- **Score each by impact potential** — low-effort, high-impact interactions rise to the top
- **Select 10–12 MTMs** for the current quarter's focus
- **Blueprint each** in detail
- **Measure and iterate** in short sprints

# Monetization Strategy

## Definition

How a seller monetizes its product — specifically, the way ownership is transferred (if at all) and the usage terms applied. The core choice in the [[Revenue Model]]. Determines pricing model, cost structure, risk allocation, and everything above in the [[Six-Layer Model Stack]].

## Three canonical strategies

Positioned along the arc of the [[Revenue Model]] (Figure 5.1):

- **[[Ownership]]** (Pay to own) — buyer pays upfront, owns the asset, takes on post-purchase risk
- **[[Subscription]]** (Pay to use) — buyer pays recurring fee to use over a period; no ownership transfer
- **[[Consumption]]** (Pay per use) — buyer pays based on actual usage, action, or outcome

And a special case:

- **[[Freemium]]** — value exchanged without direct monetary payment; monetization is indirect (data, upsell to paid tier)

## Consequences of choice

Each strategy determines:
- How [[Risk Shift|risk]] is allocated between buyer and seller
- Price points (orders of magnitude apart)
- [[Win Rate]] expectations
- [[Revenue Velocity]]
- Retention norms
- Which revenue focus dominates: selling [[Value and Impact|value]] vs delivering [[Value and Impact|impact]]

## Rules

- Under $100M ARR: **one monetization strategy**, with at most 3 GTM motions (see [[GTM Model]]).
- Changing monetization strategy is a [[Phase Shift]] — takes years, requires cultural/technological/commercial redesign. Adobe 2011–2014 is the case study.

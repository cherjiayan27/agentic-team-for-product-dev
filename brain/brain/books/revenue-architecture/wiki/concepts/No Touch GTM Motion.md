# No Touch GTM Motion

*Stub — full treatment in Ch 10. Partial definition from Ch 6.*

## Definition

The product-led / self-serve GTM motion. Customers drive the product's marketing and sales effort themselves by using the product. Equivalent to [[Product-Led Growth]] (PLG) in Jacco's framing. Not a marketing campaign, not a sales methodology — **intrinsic to the product itself**.

## Bowtie mapping (Figure 6.11)

| VM | Term |
|---|---|
| VM1 | Prospect |
| VM2 | Hand-raise (e.g. email captured for a webinar) |
| VM3 | Product Qualified Lead (PQL) — user behaviour signals conversion readiness |
| VM4 | Product Qualified Account (PQA) — multi-user account, team-license opportunity |
| VM5 | Sign-up |
| VM8 | Monthly Active Users (MAU) |

## Key characteristics (§6.4)

- **Dependency on a great product** — the product *is* the marketing tool. Beta programs are common.
- **Viral Spread** — passionate users drive organic growth; the product markets itself
- **Sign-Up Focus** — unlike other motions, the measured goal is active users, not closed logos
- **Usage sustainability is the churn signal** — MAU, WAU, DAU are critical metrics

## Applies to both application and platform sales

- **Application sales** — individual users sign up
- **Platform sales** — a user gets a fully-functional sandbox as the first step

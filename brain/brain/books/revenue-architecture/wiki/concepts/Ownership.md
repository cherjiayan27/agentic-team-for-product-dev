# Ownership

## Definition

A [[Monetization Strategy]] in which the buyer pays upfront to acquire a product outright. The buyer becomes the owner and is responsible for realising value (installing, operating, maintaining). The leftmost position on the arc of the [[Revenue Model]].

## Sub-types (Figure 5.2)

- **On-premise hardware** — servers, appliances
- **Hardware + support** — ongoing maintenance contract alongside owned hardware
- **Perpetual software** — one-time payment for indefinite-use license on a specific version

## Characteristics

- **Risk:** buyer bears it (see [[Risk Shift]])
- **Seller objective:** sell [[Value and Impact|value]] (the promise)
- **Price (1st year):** $1M+ typical for enterprise perpetual software
- **[[Win Rate]]:** 1:3 (pipeline assumption of 3x)
- **[[Revenue Velocity]]:** 9–18 months sales cycle for enterprise perpetual software
- **Retention:** ~100% (customer owns it; no renewal decision)

## Historical context

Pre-cloud B2B software dominated this category. Perpetual software deals often carried a 20% maintenance/support surcharge. Enterprise purchasing behaviour (budget approvals, vendor selection, multi-month sales cycles) is shaped by this model, which is why ownership-era pipeline math persists in SaaS organisations that shouldn't be using it.

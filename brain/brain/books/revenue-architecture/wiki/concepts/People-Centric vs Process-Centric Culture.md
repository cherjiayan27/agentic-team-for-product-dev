# People-Centric vs Process-Centric Culture

## Definition

Two organisational cultures for operating a recurring revenue business. People-centric organisations scale through staffing and optimise via individual accountability. Process-centric organisations scale through process maturity and optimise via causality analysis. Ch 4 argues process-centric is the prerequisite for the [[Recurring Revenue Factory]].

## Signs of a people-centric culture

- **Hire more when things go well.** Growth = headcount growth.
- **Fire fast when things don't.** Blame lands on individuals.
- **Opinion-based decisions.** "We think X" dominates over "the data says X."
- **Hero ball.** Over-reliance on a few exceptional performers (see [[Productivity]]).
- **Fluctuating results despite increasing investment.** The [[12 Revenue Breakpoints|repeatable-process breakpoint]] signature.

## Signs of a process-centric culture

- **When results dip, scrutinise the process first.** Not the people.
- **Data-centric decision-making.** Trendlines, cohorts, metric decomposition.
- **Causality-based culture.** "Why did this happen?" precedes "Who's responsible?"
- **Iterative improvement.** The same process gets better every quarter.
- **Process documented, not just known.** Tribal knowledge is a red flag.

## Why the distinction matters

- **Scaling dynamics:** people-centric cultures hit a ceiling (see Hero Ball + [[12 Revenue Breakpoints]] Breakpoint 5). Process-centric cultures compound.
- **Hiring strategy:** people-centric hires for heroes. Process-centric hires for process adherence.
- **AI readiness:** Factory 4.0 ([[Four Industrial Revolutions]]) requires process-centric foundations. AI multiplies processes; it doesn't multiply heroes.

## How the transition typically happens

- **Trigger:** a growth stall that can't be fixed by hiring (Breakpoint 5)
- **Realisation:** results fluctuate because the process is undocumented, not because the team is bad
- **Response:** document processes, measure them, iterate, scale

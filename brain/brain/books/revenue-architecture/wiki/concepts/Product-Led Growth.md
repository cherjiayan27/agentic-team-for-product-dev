# Product-Led Growth

## Definition

A GTM strategy in which **users experience the product before purchasing** (Dave Boyce). Not a marketing campaign, not a sales methodology, not a lead-gen tool — a full GTM motion intrinsic to the product. Equivalent to the [[No Touch GTM Motion]] in Jacco's touch framework.

## Why PLG is different

Unlike traditional GTM motions that emphasise contracts and transactional engagements, PLG emphasises **user experience and actionable usage data**. It's unambiguously customer-centric by construction.

## Seven properties that set PLG apart (§10.1.8)

1. **Comprehensive GTM approach** — engages from first touchpoint through post-purchase, delivering [[Recurring Impact]] at every interaction
2. **Customer-centric impact** — aligns company success with user success
3. **Interoperability** — PLG facilitates seamless transitions (individual → team → company)
4. **Hybrid operating model** — combines human touch with product-led self-serve
5. **Closed-loop systems via word of mouth** — advocates continuously draw new users
6. **Data-driven decision-making** — real-time segmented usage data drives feature adoption, automation, A/B testing
7. **Durable growth** — without contract lock-in, PLG naturally gravitates to its best customers (unfit customers churn fast, which is a feature not a bug)

## Developer-Led Growth (Atlassian variant)

For developer platforms, PLG is really **developer-led growth**. Converting a developer → small team → department → company takes months to years — not a bug but a feature. Developers value skill and tenure over managerial authority; their culture dictates the motion's success.

Atlassian is the canonical case. Jira/Confluence sell bottom-up through free trials and low-cost plans to individual developers, then monetise as teams scale — bypassing CxO buy-in entirely on the front end.

## Product-Led Sales (PLS)

The sibling motion. See [[Product-Led Sales]]. Combines PLG's product-usage signals with a higher-touch sales motion to close large accounts.

## Risks and caveats

- **PLG alone has a ceiling.** Most companies must blend PLG with higher-touch motions past ~$50M ARR.
- **Requires a genuinely great product.** If the product doesn't sell itself, PLG fails silently.
- **Free → paid conversion is low** (typically ~1:100 per [[Freemium]] discussion in Ch 5).

## Origin

Introduced in [[02-first-principles|Ch 2]] as one of two trends driven by the [[Risk Shift]]: letting customers prove impact to themselves mitigates seller risk in subscription businesses.

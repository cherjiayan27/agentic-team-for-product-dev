# Product-Led Sales

## Definition

**PLS.** The sales motion that tightly aligns with [[Product-Led Growth]]. A hybrid where **product-usage signals drive the sales process** — usage data tells the sales team who's ready to expand, which features resonated, and what impact users have achieved. Sits between Low Touch PLG and High Touch enterprise sales in the [[GTM Model]] (Figure 10.18).

## How PLS differs from SLG (sales-led growth)

- **SLG** — Sales team qualifies, sells, closes. Product is a feature of the pitch.
- **PLG** — Product sells itself. Sales is absent at the commit point.
- **PLS** — Product demonstrates value *first*, then a sales team enters to expand usage from pro-user/team to department/company scale.

## Where PLS fits on the GTM Model (Figure 10.18)

- **PLG** covers the Self-Serve / 1-Stage acquisition at low ACV
- **PLS** takes over in the 2-Stage / Field Sales range at medium ACV ($25K–$200K)
- **SLG (traditional)** continues above that range

PLS is essentially the *bridge* between PLG and enterprise sales.

## What enables PLS

1. **Product-usage instrumentation** — the seller must see per-user, per-feature, per-impact data
2. **User-as-champion codification** — the user's role in the decision must be explicit in the sales process
3. **Multi-stakeholder complexity management** — PLS brings the user into a broader decision, not replaces the buyer

## Key insight

Usage is a **lead-quality signal** that outperforms traditional MQL/SQL qualification by a wide margin. A PQL from a user who's already been delivered impact is a different pipeline than a cold inbound lead.

# Productivity

## Definition

On-task output per GTM rep — how effectively each individual executes proven processes. The 9th [[12 Revenue Breakpoints|revenue breakpoint]]. The gap between top and bottom performers is widening industry-wide, putting pressure on process discipline.

## Three cohorts (Ch 9 data, early 2024)

- **Normal Group (32%)** — consistent performers. Within this group, only 4.4% consistently exceed 100% of goal (vs ~20% historical B2B norm).
- **Swing Group (40%)** — achieve 50–80% of goal. Largest potential lever for improvement.
- **Bottom Group (28%)** — consistently below 50%. Do the minimum to stay employed. Historically was ~10%; grew to 28%.

## Key finding

The Normal Group outperforms the other cohorts by **1.53×** in revenue per lead. The gap is attributable primarily to **discipline in executing proven processes**, not to talent or territory.

## Four actions to improve on-task performance

1. **Shorten ramp-up time** — internal academies, call recording libraries, peer coaching, AI-driven personalised training
2. **Retain productive reps longer** — accountability culture, strengths-based deployment, promotion from within
3. **Enhance on-task performance** — continuous skill training, disciplined process execution
4. **Optimise headcount** — lowering headcount can *increase* productivity when the org has over-hired relative to process-driven capacity

## Why this matters at Scaleup → Grownup

- As companies grow, total revenue becomes the sum of per-rep productivity across large teams.
- [[Scalability]] and [[Sustainability]] both depend on productivity per dollar of salary.
- Remote work, talent-pipeline early-career bias, and coaching-manager shortage have compressed the productivity window per employee-year.

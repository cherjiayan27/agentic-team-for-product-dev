# Rational vs Emotional Impact

## Definition

Two distinct dimensions of the Impact element in [[SPICED]]. Rational Impact is quantitative and company-level; Emotional Impact is qualitative and person-level. Both drive decisions, but most GTM teams address only Rational Impact and then wonder why deals stall.

## Rational Impact (Figure 8.7)

Quantitative, measurable outcomes at the company level:

- **Increase Revenue** — shorter sales cycles, higher prices; most appealing to decision-makers
- **Reduce Cost** — efficiency-driven; may face price competition
- **Sell More** — volume increase
- **Open New Markets** — TAM expansion

Derived from "more profit" or "increase volume." Uniform across stakeholders (everyone wants more revenue or less cost).

## Emotional Impact (Figure 8.8)

Qualitative, person-level outcomes:

- **User Impact**
  - User Experience (joy of use)
  - User Interface (simplicity)
- **Buyer Impact**
  - Threat (fear of being fired)
  - Opportunity (chance of promotion)

Varies by person. Requires empathy, discovery, and pattern recognition to surface.

## Three principles (§8.2.1)

1. **Emotional Impact first benefits the person, then the company.** Rational is the inverse (savings go to company first, then employees via bonuses).
2. **Emotional Impact varies by person.** Rational is relatively uniform.
3. **Humans make emotional decisions and justify them rationally.** Product demos matter because they create emotional Impact ("I want this") which unlocks rational justification.

## The stickiness of Emotional Impact

Rational Impact **decays** as a deal ages:

- Year 1: "Your product saves us $40K/year by consolidating two $50K products."
- Year 2: customer forgets the $100K baseline; sees only the $60K line item.
- Renewal: "Why are we paying $60K for this?"

Emotional Impact **compounds** via habit formation. A product users love becomes the thing users revolt over if you try to replace it. See the Champion-leaves-revolt pattern — long-term loyalty is emotional, not rational.

## Implications

- **Discovery must uncover both.** Pure-rational discovery misses 50%+ of the decision drivers.
- **Equip the Champion.** Brief them to carry *rational* Impact to the Decider internally. Don't send them in with "this thing looks fantastic."
- **Design product for emotional stickiness.** UX simplicity compounds into durable retention.

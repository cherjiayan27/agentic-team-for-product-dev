# Recurring Impact

## Definition

The continuous, repeated delivery of value to a customer that causes them to keep paying. The precondition for [[Recurring Revenue]]: revenue recurs only because impact recurs.

## Mechanism

A customer renews when they continue to perceive that your product/service is delivering the value promised at acquisition. The moment perceived value drops below what the customer pays for, churn becomes rational.

Jacco's Netflix example: Netflix's customers pay monthly because Netflix delivers fresh content monthly. Stop delivering new content and subscribers leave within months.

## Why this matters

- Principle 5 of [[First Principles of Recurring Revenue]]: "Recurring Revenue is the result of Recurring Impact."
- [[Acquisition]] depends on *promising* impact. [[Retention]] and [[Expansion]] depend on *delivering* it.
- Organisations optimised only for the promise (sales-heavy cultures) underperform over long timeframes because the delivery half is neglected.
- Explains why customer success, product quality, and onboarding become revenue functions, not cost centres, in a subscription business.

## Empirical confirmation (Amplitude 2025)

Amplitude's 2025 benchmark report provides empirical evidence for Jacco's Principle 5: *the first-week experience directly predicts long-term retention.* Top-10% products (those delivering recurring impact early) retain users at 4–7× the median rate — see [[Retention Benchmarks (All Products)]] and [[Enterprise Retention Benchmarks]].

**Concrete translation:** "deliver recurring impact" operationalises as "win the first week." If users experience clear value within their first week, they'll keep coming back. If they don't, they're gone within three months (96% median churn rate).

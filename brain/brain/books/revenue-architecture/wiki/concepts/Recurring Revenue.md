# Recurring Revenue

## Definition

Revenue received repeatedly over time from the same customer under a subscription or subscription-like arrangement, rather than as a one-off payment. The defining revenue model of SaaS and modern subscription businesses.

## Core property

**Recurring revenue is the outcome of [[Recurring Impact]]**, not a pricing choice alone. A customer only continues paying while they continue receiving value. This is Principle 5 of the [[First Principles of Recurring Revenue]].

## Contrast: perpetual / upfront-payment model

- **Perpetual:** customer pays once, owns the asset, bears the risk of installation/operation/failure. Seller's profit is realised at sale time.
- **Recurring:** customer pays repeatedly, seller bears the risk of continuously delivering value. Seller's profit accrues over years.
- The [[Risk Shift]] is the mechanism behind this difference.

## Consequences

- Growth decomposes into [[Three Components of Growth]] — not one.
- Profit takes years to compound (Principle 3 of [[First Principles of Recurring Revenue]]).
- Customer-centricity stops being a nice-to-have and becomes a revenue-engine requirement — see [[Customer-Centric Growth]].
- Retention and expansion become more strategically important than acquisition past ~$10M ARR — see [[Four Stages of Growth]].

# Recurring vs Re-occurring Revenue

## Definition

A precise distinction Jacco insists on: **recurring** revenue is systematic, predictable, regular. **Re-occurring** revenue is sporadic — it happens again but without a consistent schedule or amount. The distinction drives valuation, not just semantics.

## Recurring revenue

- Happens repeatedly **and** consistently
- Predictable pattern at regular intervals
- Examples: monthly SaaS subscription billed on the 1st, annual SaaS renewal
- Customers have ongoing access to the product/service conditional on continued payment
- Drives higher valuation multiples

## Re-occurring revenue

- Happens again but lacks regularity or predictability
- Amount and frequency can vary
- Examples:
  - **On-prem support contracts** — may involve recurring payments but primarily cover maintenance for physical devices (re-occurring, not recurring)
  - **Insertion orders (ad tech)** — one-time agreements; if budget is spent regularly within the order, it's re-occurring
- Often mislabeled as "recurring" by companies seeking higher valuation

## The real test (§5.3.4)

It's **not about whether payments happen repeatedly.** It's about whether the customer has **ongoing access to the product or service conditional on continued payment**, creating a regular and predictable stream. Mere repetition of invoices isn't sufficient.

## Q&A from the chapter

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| On-prem support contract = recurring? | No — re-occurring |
| Ad tech insertion order = subscription? | No — if budget is recurring, it's re-occurring / consumption |
| Usage-based consumption = subscription? | Yes — recurring (customer pays for ongoing access/use) |

## Why it matters

- Valuation: [[Recurring Revenue]] businesses command significantly higher multiples. Adobe is $150B on $17B ARR at 8.8x.
- Strategy: a re-occurring revenue pattern tells you customers aren't continuously hooked on the product's impact — a sign the [[Revenue Model]] is wrong or the [[Recurring Impact]] isn't being delivered.

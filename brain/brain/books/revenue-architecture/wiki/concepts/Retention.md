# Retention

## Definition

Revenue retained from **existing customers** through renewal — i.e. the portion of prior-period revenue that is not lost to churn. One of the [[Three Components of Growth]] in a recurring revenue business.

## Properties

- **Dependency:** on the organisation's ability to *deliver* value continuously — see [[Recurring Impact]].
- **Cost profile:** approximately **10% of retained revenue** in Jacco's baseline assumption. Dramatically lower than [[Acquisition]] cost.
- **Growth curve:** hyperbolic / polynomial. Typically lags [[Acquisition]] by about one year in the first 5 years, then exceeds it around **$10M ARR**.
- **Dominance:** central to Stage 2 of the [[Four Stages of Growth]]. Becomes the primary engine of growth beyond $10M ARR.

## Why it matters

- Retention-driven growth has a much better unit economics profile than acquisition-driven growth.
- Over-reliance on acquisition past $10M ARR is a strategic error — see [[First Principles of Recurring Revenue]] Principle 2.
- Typically takes ~2 years to shift an organisation from acquisition-focused to retention-focused. Starting that transition early (e.g. at $6–8M ARR) is strongly advantaged.

## Risk

Reassigning the acquisition (sales) team to handle retention typically pushes retention cost from ~10% up toward acquisition-level costs (~100%), collapsing long-run profitability. Figure 2.7 in the source shows this failure mode.

## Revenue retention vs user retention

Jacco's Retention is **revenue-based** (GRR, NRR — see [[Gross Revenue Retention]] / [[Net Revenue Retention]]). Amplitude's 2025 benchmark report measures **user retention** — the % of users who return in a given month — see [[User Retention]]. The two are complementary:

- **User retention** = leading indicator (visible in weeks)
- **Revenue retention** = lagging indicator (visible at renewal)

Amplitude's empirical data (see [[Retention Benchmarks (All Products)]]) shows top-10% products achieve 4–5× better user retention than the median, with enterprise segments showing **~7× gaps** between top 10% and median at month 3.

# Risk Shift

## Definition

The transfer of purchase risk from buyer to seller when a business moves from a perpetual/upfront-payment revenue model to a subscription-based recurring revenue model. Principle 4 of the [[First Principles of Recurring Revenue]].

## Mechanism

### Under perpetual / ownership model
- Buyer pays upfront, typically with a 20% surcharge for a maintenance/support contract.
- Buyer assumes risk on receipt: unpacking, installing, operating, handling failure.
- Seller's profit is realised at the moment of sale.

### Under recurring / subscription model
- Seller invests upfront in infrastructure, onboarding, and ongoing support — often before meaningful revenue is recognised.
- Seller must continue to deliver updates, uptime, and value every period or lose the customer.
- Seller's profit accrues over years as customers renew and expand (see [[First Principles of Recurring Revenue]] Principle 3).

## Consequences

- **Customer-centricity is no longer optional.** Early churn is catastrophic because payback periods for [[Acquisition]] cost are measured in years. See [[Customer-Centric Growth]].
- **Quality-over-quantity outreach.** Organisations qualify hard for customers who can actually achieve the promised impact.
- **[[Product-Led Growth]].** Letting customers self-qualify through early product use mitigates seller risk because the buyer has experienced impact before committing.
- **Buyer expectations lag the model.** Buyers in 2023 still expect 20%-style discounts even though the risk burden has flipped. Cultural/purchasing habits trail the economic reality.

## Ch 5 extension (Figure 5.6)

Ch 5 generalises the risk shift across the full [[Revenue Model]] arc, not just the binary perpetual-vs-subscription case:

- **[[Ownership]]** — buyer bears all risk (post-purchase installation, operation, failure)
- **[[Subscription]]** — balanced; seller commits to product development, infrastructure, continuous value delivery
- **[[Consumption]]** — seller bears all risk (builds global infrastructure before revenue materialises; buyer pays only for what works)

Overall risk across the system actually *decreases* in cloud-era subscription/consumption models because infrastructure is shared — but the seller's share of that smaller total is much larger.

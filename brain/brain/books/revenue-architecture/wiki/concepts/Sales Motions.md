# Sales Motions

## Definition

The five canonical ways a sales organisation engages a customer, from fully self-serve to high-touch dedicated teams. Each motion has a specific cost profile and revenue expectation. Overlays on the [[GTM Model]] X-Y framework.

## The five (Figure 10.5)

1. **Self-Service** — customers purchase without human contact. Credit-card signups (Shopify, Dropbox, Canva).
2. **Inside Sales (1-Stage)** — inbound; customer mostly drives themselves, reaches out with questions. Pro-user licenses (Adobe Creative Cloud individual).
3. **Inside Sales (2-Stage)** — SDR qualifies → AE closes. Outbound-heavy. Procore-style vertical SaaS.
4. **Field Sales** — reps living near customers; online meetings augmented with in-person visits. Standard for medium-to-high value deals.
5. **Named-Account Sales** — complex multi-stakeholder deals in large organisations; salespeople act as consultants.

## Cost vs quota (Table 10.1)

| Motion | Cost (salary + expense) | Quota/year | Cost-ratio jump |
|---|---|---|---|
| Self-Service | $0 (goes to marketing) | n/a | — |
| Inside Sales 1-stage | $45K–$60K | $240K–$480K | ∞ vs self |
| Inside Sales 2-stage | $150K–$250K | $480K–$960K | **5×** |
| Field Sales | $250K–$500K | $720K–$1.4M | **4×** |
| Field + SE | $350K–$800K | $960K–$2M | **3×** |
| Named-Account Team | $600K–$2M | $1M–$10M | **2×** |

## Key insight: cost doesn't scale linearly

Each step up in sales motion multiplies cost by 2–5× but multiplies quota by a smaller factor. The only way the math works is if ACV also multiplies — which is why sales motion must align with the x-axis of the [[GTM Model]].

## Composition of touch motions

Each of the [[5 Touch GTM Motions]] typically includes one sales motion as its commercial engine. See [[Touch Concept]] for how Sales combines with [[Marketing Motions]] and [[Customer Success Motions]].

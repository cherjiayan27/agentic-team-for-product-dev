# Scalability

## Definition

Growth as a function of **velocity** — how quickly the company can repeat and extend what works. The 7th [[12 Revenue Breakpoints|revenue breakpoint]]. Distinct from [[Sustainability]] (which adds cost to the picture).

## Key thesis

**First to scale > first to market.** Many dominant companies weren't first movers (LinkedIn faced Plaxo; Slack wasn't the first team chat) but were first to scale a focused, mature GTM motion.

## The ACME vs NewCo case study (Figure 9.14)

- **ACME** (2015) — raised Series A, experimented across three GTM motions (High Touch $5M ARR, Low Touch $3M ARR, PLG $2M ARR). Spread its $25M C-round across all three. None were mature enough to scale.
- **NewCo** (2016, 2 years later) — focused on one GTM motion (enterprise High Touch). Raised $35M after hitting $10M ARR. Tripled resources on a single mature motion.
- **Result:** NewCo reached $100M ARR first despite starting later. Velocity advantage.

## The scaleup imperative

Scaleups must:
1. Complete breakpoints 1–6 ([[GTM Fit]]) on at least one motion
2. **Resist diversifying prematurely.** Launching a second motion before the first is mature fractures the scaling investment.
3. Pair funding with focus — scaling requires replicating *what works*, not experimenting with *what might*.

## Velocity vs speed

- **Speed** = raw pace
- **Velocity** = speed + direction. In Jacco's terms, Scalability encompasses both — how fast you grow AND whether you're growing in a direction that compounds

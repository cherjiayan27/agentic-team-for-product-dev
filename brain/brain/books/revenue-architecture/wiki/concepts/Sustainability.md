# Sustainability

## Definition

Growth maintained at a manageable cost over time. The 8th [[12 Revenue Breakpoints|revenue breakpoint]]. Where [[Scalability]] asks "can we repeat it?", Sustainability asks "can we afford to?"

## The cost asymmetry problem

During an economic downturn, all GTM metrics decline simultaneously (fewer leads, lower CRs, more discounts, higher churn, less expansion). **Costs don't decrease in proportion to revenue.** This is the signature of an unsustainable motion.

## Worked example (Tables 9.5–9.7)

A Low Touch GTM motion with an SDR ($80K OTE) + AE ($140K OTE) selling a $20K platform:

### Q1 (good market)
- 2.3 deals/month → $41,310 MRR → **$495,720 ARR**
- Acquisition cost: $225,058 (salaries + campaign)
- **CAC = 45% of ARR**

### Q5 (economic downturn)
- All CRs decline ~5% across the board
- 1.1 deals/month → $19,094 MRR → **$229,133 ARR**
- Acquisition cost: $164,409 (variable comp drops with performance)
- **CAC = 72% of ARR** — almost doubled as a percentage

## Why this matters

- **Volatility works both ways.** The same [[Nonlinearity]] that produces [[Hypergrowth]] in upswings produces disproportionate cost-intensity drops.
- **Fixed costs don't flex.** Variable comp does, but base salaries, tooling, and infrastructure don't proportionally.
- **Narrow-margin motions die first.** If your motion was at 90% CAC in a good quarter, it's underwater in a normal one.

## Key takeaway for GTM teams

Monitor each motion on **two** axes monthly:
- **Scalability** (via Growth Formula): is the process producing the expected revenue per lead?
- **Sustainability** (via CAC / CTS ratios): is the cost per dollar of revenue manageable?

Both must be tracked per motion, not averaged across the business.

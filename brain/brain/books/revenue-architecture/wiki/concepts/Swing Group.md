# Swing Group

## Definition

The cohort of GTM representatives who consistently achieve **50–80% of their goals**. Typically ~40% of the team. Named by Jacco because they have the **most significant potential to swing the organisation's overall performance** — not the top performers, but the middle band.

## Why Swing Group matters most

- **Top performers** (Normal Group, 32%) are already maxed out in discipline and execution. Small improvement per rep.
- **Bottom performers** (Bottom Group, 28%) are structurally disengaged. Hard to move.
- **Swing Group** is large *and* movable. A 10% lift across 40% of the team produces more revenue than trying to squeeze another 5% from the top cohort.

## Operational focus

When running a productivity program, invest training and coaching **primarily in the Swing Group**. Not glamorous, but statistically the largest leverage point.

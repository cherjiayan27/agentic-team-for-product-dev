# T2D3 Growth Trend

## Definition

Neeraj Agrawal's (Battery Ventures, 2015) canonical growth trajectory for successful SaaS startups during the Golden Era. After reaching $1M ARR, the company:

- **Triples** (T): $1.4M → $4.2M
- **Triples** (T): $4.2M → $12.6M
- **Doubles** (D): $12.6M → $25.2M
- **Doubles** (D): $25.2M → $50.4M
- **Doubles** (D): $50.4M → $100.8M

Five years post-$1M-ARR → ~$100M ARR → unicorn status at historical valuation multiples.

## Why it was reliable

- Correlation rooted in the same underlying mechanics (S-curve + Compound Effect + Denominator Effect) that Jacco now treats as causal.
- Predictability is a signal that GTM processes have matured and the company has met market demand.
- Companies hitting T2D3 historically secured large late-stage funding rounds at premium valuations.

## Why it's less reliable now

- Golden Era multiples ended with the 2022 SaaS crash
- [[Hypergrowth]] rates above $10M ARR have compressed across the industry
- Process-maturity bar has risen — investors now scrutinise repeatability, not just growth rate

## How to use

T2D3 works as a **historical reference** for sanity-checking trajectory:
- Are we at 3x YoY at $3M ARR? (approximates CMGR ~10%)
- Are we at 2x YoY at $30M ARR? (approximates CMGR ~6%)

Not as a **target** — treating T2D3 as a target leads to Blitzscaling behaviours that break unit economics.

## Prerequisite

T2D3-like growth needs:
1. One primary GTM motion (not five), with
2. Mature repeatable processes (typically past $8M ARR per motion), and
3. A growing market

# Think Big, Act Small, Move Fast

## Definition

The deployment philosophy for Revenue Architecture. Three modes executed concurrently:

1. **Think Big** — industrial-scale vision using the 4 core dynamic models
2. **Act Small** — execute via [[Moments That Matter]], not transformation programs
3. **Move Fast** — short sprints, measure, iterate, do more of what works

Balances ambition (Think Big) with execution discipline (Act Small) and adaptive learning (Move Fast).

## Think Big

Deploy the four dynamic models in this sequence (Figure 11.1):

1. **[[Growth Model]]** — where are you on the S-curve? Which of the [[12 Revenue Breakpoints]] are achieved, missed, or in progress?
2. **[[GTM Model]]** — which GTM motions align with the growth plan? Which should you invest in?
3. **[[Data Model]]** — transition each motion from funnel to [[Bowtie]]; enable scenario analysis
4. **[[Operating Model]]** — identify and blueprint the [[Moments That Matter]]

Steward model: assign an executive per chapter/model; they become the implementation leader for that domain.

## Act Small

Not "boil the ocean" transformation. Identify 10–12 [[Moments That Matter]] per quarter; blueprint each; improve them 5–10% at a time. [[Compound Impact]] across MTMs produces system-wide results.

## Move Fast

Short sprints, usually quarterly:

- Pick an MTM to improve
- Run a small-scale experiment
- Measure leading indicators
- If it works, roll it out broadly
- If it doesn't, stop and learn
- Next sprint

Avoid multi-year transformations. Avoid long deliberations. Quick iteration beats perfect planning.

## Why this sequence

- **Think Big without Act Small** → paralysis, over-planning, abstract vision without execution
- **Act Small without Think Big** → incremental gains in the wrong direction
- **Move Fast without Act Small** → chaos; random experiments
- **All three together** → a focused, disciplined execution machine

## Watchmaker analogy

Customer-facing simplicity requires deep engineering underneath. The simplicity of glancing at a watch hides hundreds of precision components. Revenue Architecture deployment is the same: the customer experience looks simple; the revenue factory underneath is sophisticated.

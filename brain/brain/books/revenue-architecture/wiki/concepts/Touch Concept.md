# Touch Concept

## Definition

A framework for quantifying the level of customer engagement across the GTM organisation. Originally from David Skok (2010) as a way to describe sales complexity cost. Jacco extends it from sales-only to unify **marketing + sales + customer success** into coherent motions at each touch level.

> *"Touch levels provide a measurable standard for human effort in the sales process, allowing for resource allocation and strategy alignment." — David Skok*

## Why it's a unifying framework

Before the Touch Concept, GTM vocabulary was fragmented:

- **Market segmentation** (VSB, SMB, Mid-Market, Enterprise) describes *who* you target
- **Marketing/sales strategy** (inbound, SDR/AE) describes *how* each function operates
- **GTM approach** (low touch, high touch) describes *engagement level*

Overlaps caused confusion — e.g. an outbound strategy can target SMB or a division within an Enterprise. The Touch Concept resolves this by **making engagement level the organising axis**, with marketing/sales/CS motions all falling out from it.

## The five touch levels

See [[5 Touch GTM Motions]] for the full matrix of marketing × sales × CS at each level.

- **No Touch** — fully self-serve; community-driven support
- **Low Touch** — event-based marketing; volume-based CS
- **Medium Touch** — personalised sales; account-centric marketing
- **High Touch** — custom marketing; deeply engaged CS
- **Dedicated Touch** — specialised team solely serving one account (e.g. Disney with ESPN/Disney Parks divisions)

## Musical metaphor

Jacco's framing: the Touch Concept assembles musicians playing different instruments (marketing, sales, CS) into a symphony — *all playing from the same music sheets*. Without the touch concept, each function plays its own song.

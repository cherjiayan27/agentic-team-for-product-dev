# Value and Impact

## Definition

A complementary pair. **Value** is the *promise* a seller makes about future benefit. **Impact** is the *fulfilment* of that promise — the tangible, measurable result the customer experiences during use.

> Value represents a promise of future Impact.  
> Impact is the fulfilment of the promised Value.  
> — *Revenue Architecture*, §5.2.1

## Value

- Stated in the value proposition, marketing, sales pitch
- Articulated as "enhanced efficiency, streamlined communication" etc.
- Aligned with [[Ownership]] monetization — buyer pays for the promise, then it's their job to realize the benefit
- Precondition for [[Acquisition]]

## Impact

- Measured post-purchase as actual outcome (e.g. "30% reduction in project completion time")
- Aligned with [[Subscription]] and [[Consumption]] monetization — buyer pays continuously for delivered impact
- Precondition for [[Retention]] and [[Expansion]]
- Synonymous with [[Recurring Impact]] when delivered continuously

## The chasm

Jacco's Figure 5.5 shows a significant chasm on the [[Revenue Model]] arc between value-based (Ownership) and impact-based (Subscription/Consumption) monetization. Business practices, metrics, and culture do not transfer cleanly across this chasm. A company shifting from ownership to subscription without rebuilding on the Impact side of the chasm will struggle. Adobe's 2011–2014 [[Phase Shift]] is the canonical successful example of bridging it.

## Why this distinction matters

- Promise (Value) wins [[Acquisition]]; delivery (Impact) wins [[Retention]] and [[Expansion]].
- Metrics tied only to Value (e.g. deals closed, revenue booked) drive seller behaviour that damages long-term Impact and collapses [[Retention]].
- [[First Principles of Recurring Revenue]] Principle 5: Recurring Revenue = Recurring Impact.

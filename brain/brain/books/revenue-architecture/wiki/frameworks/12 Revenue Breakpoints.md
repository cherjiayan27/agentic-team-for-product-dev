# 12 Revenue Breakpoints

## Definition

The twelve predictable hurdles every recurring revenue business encounters on the way from $1M ARR → $1B ARR. Apply per GTM motion and must be re-navigated when launching new motions, new segments, or new regions. Companies rarely fail because of the product — they fail because they miss one of these.

## The 12 breakpoints (Figure 9.13)

### Part 1: PMF → GTMF (first six)

| # | Breakpoint | What kills you if you miss it |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Pricing and Packaging** | Mixed monetization strategies at $100M ARR; revenue-recognition confusion; refund-policy ambiguity |
| 2 | **Founder-Led Growth** | Over-reliance on the founder; process bypass; can't scale beyond founder's personal capacity |
| 3 | **Data Model and Structure** | Ill-defined funnel, departmental conflicts over data validity, no [[Bowtie]] adoption, no benchmarking possible |
| 4 | **GTM Model selection** | Running the wrong motion for the deal size; spawning 20+ uncoordinated motions at $250M ARR |
| 5 | **Repeatable Process** | "Hero ball" — relying on exceptional individuals; fluctuating results despite more spend; premature S-curve plateau |
| 6 | **[[Growth Formula]] + [[Unit Economics]]** | Can't predict or plan resource needs per motion; investment decisions become opinion-based |

### Part 2: GTMF → Revenue Factory (next six)

| # | Breakpoint | What kills you if you miss it |
|---|---|---|
| 7 | **Velocity ([[Scalability]])** | Spreading investment across too many immature motions (ACME example) vs concentrating on one (NewCo example); first-to-scale > first-to-market |
| 8 | **[[Sustainability]] (Cost of Growth)** | CAC rises disproportionately during downturns; costs don't shrink as fast as revenue contracts |
| 9 | **[[Productivity]]** | Growing [[Swing Group]] and bottom cohort; lower ramp, higher attrition, insufficient on-task execution |
| 10 | **Interoperability** | Tool stack fragmentation; data silos between marketing/sales/CS; AI closed loops impossible |
| 11 | **Durability** | Can't weather multi-year headwinds; brand issues, regulatory shocks, competitive disruption (e.g. OpenAI Dec 2022) |
| 12 | **Profitability** | Failing the Rule of 40 (growth % + profit margin ≥ 40%); becoming a zombie public company |

## Why breakpoints repeat

Each breakpoint applies **per GTM motion**. Launching a new motion (e.g. moving from PLG to enterprise field sales) means re-navigating breakpoints 1–6 for the new motion. Fast-growing companies are re-fighting earlier breakpoints while simultaneously navigating later ones.

## The area of focus

Figure 9.6 highlights that **breakpoints 1–6 sit in the "area of focus"** — the zone where management attention produces disproportionate results. After breakpoint 6 (Growth Formula), execution discipline takes over.

## Common failure mode: accelerating through breakpoints

Companies with a C-round often have only implemented a handful of repeatable processes. The capital pushes them to scale before maturity — which is why the Scaleup → Grownup transition is the one most companies fail.

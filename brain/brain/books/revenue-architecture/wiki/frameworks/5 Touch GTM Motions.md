# 5 Touch GTM Motions

## Definition

The five canonical GTM motions organised by **touch level** — the amount of human engagement per customer. Unifies [[Sales Motions]], [[Marketing Motions]], and [[Customer Success Motions]] into one coherent operating style per motion. Concept originally from David Skok (2010), extended by Jacco.

## The five motions (Figure 10.8, Table 10.2)

| Motion | ACV | Engine | Marketing | Sales | CS | What people talk about | How it spreads |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **No Touch** | $0–$5K | Product | Self-Serve + Inbound | None (self-purchase) | Community | User experience | Word of mouth |
| **Low Touch** | $5K–$15K | Velocity | Inbound | Inside Sales 1-Stage | Helpdesk, Volume CS | Easy to deploy | Word of mouth |
| **Medium Touch** | $15K–$50K | Content | Outbound | Inside Sales 2-Stage (SDR + AE) | Volume CS | Thought leadership | Events / webinars |
| **High Touch** | $50K–$500K | Brand / Expertise | Targeted / ABM | Field Sales | Vertical CS | Expertise of people | Networking |
| **Dedicated Touch** | >$500K | Relationships | Networking + CxO ABM | Named-Account Team | Account-Based CS | Responsiveness | CxO → CxO referral |

## Cost to serve (Figure 10.10)

Per-customer first-year cost **scales exponentially, not linearly**:

- No Touch: ~$10
- Low Touch: ~$100
- Medium Touch: ~$1,000
- High Touch: ~$10,000
- Dedicated Touch: $100,000+

Each tier is a **10×** jump. This is why aligning ACV with motion is non-negotiable.

## Each motion has a different engine

Motions can't be swapped like interchangeable parts. They run on fundamentally different principles:

- **No Touch runs on product.** Without a great product delivering impact instantly, there's nothing.
- **Low Touch runs on velocity.** Fast signup → fast deploy → fast value.
- **Medium Touch runs on content.** Without thought leadership, nothing attracts the SDR/AE pipeline.
- **High Touch runs on brand + expert people.** A trusted name and senior SMEs.
- **Dedicated Touch runs on CxO relationships.** No relationship, no deal.

Choosing the motion means committing to building that engine. Trying to run High Touch with a content-marketing team (no relationships) fails. Trying to run Low Touch with a field sales team (no velocity) fails.

## Interoperability

Multiple motions can coexist — but **only if structured interoperability is built into the [[Operating Model]]**. See [[Interoperability]]. Running 2+ motions without shared [[Data Model]] conventions creates failures at $20–50M ARR.

## Selecting the right motion

Use the X-Y framework of the [[GTM Model]]:

1. Locate your ACV on the x-axis
2. Locate your expected deal volume on the y-axis
3. Read off the motion — that's where the GTM Model tells you to focus

**Exceptions matter:** if NRR expands the effective ACV (e.g. $40K → $400K over 3 years), the right motion is defined by the *multi-year* ACV, not year 1 (see Use Case 4 in Ch 10.2.4).

# Channels

## Definition

The four types of third-party partnerships a company can leverage alongside direct-sales GTM motions. Each plays a distinct role and can apply to any part of the [[Bowtie]] customer journey.

## The four types (Figure 10.9)

| Channel | Role | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| **Affiliates** | *Promote* your offering (commission-based) | Commission on referred sales |
| **Resellers** | *Sell* your offering | 10% (referral) to 50%+ (VAR with volume commit, direct salesforce, first-line support) |
| **Strategic Partners** | *Enhance* your offering (complementary tech/services) | Usually revenue-sharing or joint GTM |
| **System Integrators (SI)** | *Integrate* your offering into complex/compliance-sensitive stacks | Professional-services fees; often embedded in the customer's delivery budget |

## When to use each

- **Affiliates** — high-volume/low-cost; good for No/Low Touch motions targeting pros with influencer networks
- **Resellers** — geographic or language expansion; access to established SMB customer bases
- **Strategic Partners** — platform plays where interoperability creates joint value (e.g. AWS partner ecosystem)
- **System Integrators** — mandatory when selling into regulated sectors (Department of Defense → SOC/FISMA compliance)

## Where they sit in the customer journey (Figure 10.9)

- **Affiliates** — Awareness / Education
- **Strategic Partners** — Selection / Commit / Onboarding
- **Resellers** — Selection / Commit / Onboarding / Retention
- **System Integrators** — Commit / Onboarding (integration phase)

## Common failure

Treating channels as a GTM motion substitute rather than a supplement. A weak direct motion doesn't get fixed by adding a reseller — the reseller inherits the same broken unit economics.

# Four Types of Expansion

## Definition

A 2×2 matrix of expansion motions, distinguished by two axes: **Impact** (existing vs new) and **Beneficiary** (existing vs new). Maps to CR8 in the [[Bowtie Metrics]]. Understanding the four quadrants clarifies which team should handle which expansion type — a common [[Operating Model]] mistake is assigning all four to one team.

## The 2×2 (Figure 6.5)

|   | **Beneficiary: Existing** | **Beneficiary: New** |
|---|---|---|
| **Impact: Existing** | **Renew** — sell the same impact to the same group | **Resell** — resell a new [[Champion]] on the same impact |
| **Impact: New** | **Upsell** — sell a new product to the same group providing more impact | **Cross-sell** — sell another group within the same company on impact |

## Definitions and who owns them

### Upsell
- Sell additional products, features, higher tiers, or more seats to the same benefactor
- Includes geographic expansion and annual price increases
- **Who handles it:** CSM reactively, but proactive upsell needs an Account Manager

### Cross-sell
- Sell to a *different* group/department/business unit within the same customer (e.g. ESPN + Disney Parks + ABC are three customers inside The Walt Disney Company)
- Often requires unseating a competitor chosen by a different Champion
- **Who handles it:** Never CSMs — this is the most complicated sale. Requires an acquisition-focused salesperson.

### Renew
- Same impact, same beneficiary. The default annual renewal cycle.
- Driven by continued [[Recurring Impact]], low-friction renewal process, responsive support
- **Who handles it:** CSMs and auto-renewal processes

### Resell
- New Champion (the existing one left, was promoted, or moved companies), same impact
- Most overlooked quadrant, yet carries both the biggest threat (new person wants to cut costs) and biggest opportunity (your old Champion might introduce you at their new company)
- **Who handles it:** Must be reprioritised immediately. Often goes to the Account Manager + a follow-up to the former Champion.

## Why separation matters

- **Cost profiles differ.** Renewal cost ~10% of retained revenue. Cross-sell cost approaches acquisition cost (100%). Blending teams blends cost ratios and collapses NRR economics — see [[Retention]] risk section.
- **Skills differ.** Cross-sell = hunting. Renew = farming. Different competencies; mixing them produces mediocre performance at both.
- **Compensation differs.** Cross-sell and Upsell need acquisition-like incentives; Renew can run on retention-style comp.

## Impact on Lifetime Value

All four feed [[Net Revenue Retention]] and [[Lifetime Value]]. Getting the expansion motion correctly segmented is how the right-side revenue engine of the [[Bowtie]] produces [[Hypergrowth]].

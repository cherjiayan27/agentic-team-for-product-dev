# GTM Model

## Definition

Layer 6 of the [[Six-Layer Model Stack]] — the topmost, most dynamic layer. An X-Y framework that aligns **marketing, sales, and customer success** across five **touch-based GTM motions**. Plots each motion against two axes: ACV (x) and annual deal volume (y). Each motion defines a coherent way to acquire, retain, and expand customers at a specific scale.

## The X-Y framework

- **X-axis — Annual Contract Value** (categorical breakpoints around $5K, $15K, $50K, $500K, $1M+)
- **Y-axis — Deals per year** (logarithmic: 1s, 10s, 100s, 1Ks, 10Ks, 100Ks)

Why both axes matter:
- At $50/year ACV, you need 100,000 customers to reach $5M ARR
- At $500K/year ACV, you need 10 customers to reach $5M ARR
- These are categorically different businesses — different marketing, sales, and CS motions.

## The five touch motions (Figure 10.8)

See [[5 Touch GTM Motions]] for full detail. Summary:

| Motion | ACV range | Engine (Table 10.2) |
|---|---|---|
| No Touch | $0–$5K | Product (UX) |
| Low Touch | $5K–$15K | Velocity |
| Medium Touch | $15K–$50K | Content |
| High Touch | $50K–$500K | Brand / Expertise |
| Dedicated Touch | >$500K | Relationships |

## Why it matters

- **Prevents the #1 failure mode.** Most GTM failures come from running the wrong motion for your ACV/volume intersection — or the right motion poorly.
- **Aligns three functions.** Without the GTM Model, marketing, sales, and CS run in silos with conflicting terminology.
- **Enables [[Scenario Analysis]]** across motions: "what happens if we add a High Touch motion to our current Low Touch?"
- **Forces cost discipline.** Cost to serve one customer scales **exponentially** across motions (~$10 → $100 → $1K → $10K → $100K+). Cross-motion comparison without the GTM Model hides this asymmetry.

## Relationship to the stack

- Depends on [[Revenue Model]] (monetization strategy affects achievable touch motion)
- Depends on [[Data Model]] (Bowtie maps each motion's journey)
- Depends on [[Mathematical Model]] (Growth Formula per motion)
- Depends on [[Growth Model]] (motion selection evolves across Growth Stages)
- Depends on [[Operating Model]] (structures the revenue team that executes the motions)

## Governance

- **≤3 motions until $50M ARR.** Then introduce a new product to break the $100M barrier.
- **One [[Growth Formula]] per motion.** Never average across motions.
- **[[Interoperability]] is a first-class concern** whenever >1 motion runs.

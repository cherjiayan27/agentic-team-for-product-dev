# Growth Model

## Definition

Layer 5 of the [[Six-Layer Model Stack]]. A composite framework for understanding, predicting, and operating growth in a recurring revenue business. Built from five components that together describe where a company is on its trajectory and what happens next.

## The five components

| Component | What it tells you |
|---|---|
| [[S-Curve\|Growth Pattern]] | Most subscription businesses trace an S-curve; recognising the pattern in your own data reveals whether you're pre-inflection, compounding, or plateauing |
| [[Annual Recurring Revenue\|Growth Rate]] | Annual, CAGR, CMGR — the speed of compounding |
| [[Growth Stages]] | Seed → Startup → Scaleup → Grownup → Enterprise; each tied to funding, time, and expected growth rate |
| Growth Trends | Historical trends like [[T2D3 Growth Trend]]; reflect the maturity of underlying GTM processes |
| Growth Funding Stages | A-round → B-round → C-round → D-round → IPO; survival rates drop ~50% per round |

## Why it's operational, not just analytical

Growth modelling is **not a valuation metric** — it's an operational tool that:

- **Forecasts future revenue** from historical data assuming process maturity holds
- **Plans resources 12–18 months ahead** — human processes take 1–2 years to mature
- **Flags [[Inflection Point]]s** early so leadership can choose expansion vs profitability
- **Decomposes total revenue into per-motion [[Growth Formula]]s** — each GTM motion is its own revenue production line

## Relationship to other layers

- Built on [[Mathematical Model]] (uses [[Growth Formula]] per motion)
- Built on [[Data Model]] (uses [[Bowtie Metrics]])
- Built on [[Revenue Model]] (monetization determines achievable growth rates)
- Feeds into [[GTM Model]] (growth strategy chooses which motions to scale)

## Core operating rules

- **One Growth Formula per GTM motion**, aggregated into overall growth (see [[Growth Formula]])
- **Process maturity is the hidden driver.** Companies don't grow as fast as their product merits; they grow as fast as their GTM processes mature.
- **Rapid growth requires pre-investment.** If you wait until you need the growth to invest in capacity, you miss the window.
- **Watch [[12 Revenue Breakpoints]].** They explain why most funded companies fail even with a working product.

## Why investors prefer subscription

Combining [[Compound Impact]] + [[Denominator Effect]] + [[S-Curve]] produces the **predictable hyperbolic growth** investors love — much more attractive than linear ownership revenue or spiky consumption revenue.

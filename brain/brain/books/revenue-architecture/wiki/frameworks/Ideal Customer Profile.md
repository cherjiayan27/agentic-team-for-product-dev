# Ideal Customer Profile

## Definition

**ICP.** The canonical description of the customer most likely to achieve recurring [[Value and Impact|Impact]] from your product — and therefore the customer worth acquiring, retaining, and expanding. Central to Account-Based Marketing (ABM) and a prerequisite for [[Durability]].

## What an ICP contains

Traditional ICPs list firmographics (industry, size, geography, revenue). A SPICED-era ICP extends this with:

- **Situation** — the specific context in which the customer operates
- **Pain** — the problem the product addresses
- **Impact potential** — the rational and emotional outcomes the customer can reasonably expect

This is why Jacco positions ICP as "match to the target profile based on Situation, Pain, and Impact potential" (see [[Bowtie Metrics]] VM1).

## Why it matters

- **Acquisition quality.** ICP-match customers have higher win rates and lower churn.
- **Retention signal.** Customers outside your ICP typically churn earliest — they never had the conditions to receive Impact.
- **[[Durability]] prerequisite.** The Durability stage of [[GTM Maturity Stages]] requires ICP discipline: qualify for customers who can actually achieve the promised outcome.
- **Expansion vector.** Within the [[Four Types of Expansion]], Cross-sell depends on understanding the ICP across divisions/departments.

## Using ICP with ABM

In [[Marketing Motions|Targeted/ABM motions]], ICP feeds directly into:

- **Target account selection** — which companies to pursue
- **[[Buying Center]] mapping** — who the personas are within each target
- **Messaging customisation** — what Rational/Emotional Impact to emphasise per persona

## Failure mode

Selling outside ICP. When a seller (or a CRO under pressure) closes deals outside the ICP to hit a quota, the result is:
- Lower win rate on the bad-fit deals (more cycles wasted)
- Higher churn (customer can't achieve Impact)
- CS burden to support non-ideal customers
- Brand damage from publicly-visible bad-fit case studies

# Mathematical Model

## Definition

Layer 3 of the [[Six-Layer Model Stack]] — the causality layer. Models the non-linear behaviour of a recurring revenue business using the mathematics of repeated multiplication ([[Compound Impact]]) and accumulation over time. The blueprint that explains *why* small changes in conversion rates or retention produce disproportionate outcomes in revenue.

## Central insight

A recurring revenue business is **not linear**. Two mathematical domains operate simultaneously (see [[Two Domains of the Bowtie]]):

- **[[Acquisition]]** — governed by Π (product of conversion rates). An *open-loop* system. Sensitive to marginal changes across multiple sub-systems.
- **[[Retention]] and [[Expansion]]** — governed by Σ over time (accumulation). A *closed-loop* system. Sensitive to rates above or below 1.

## Metrics formalised in this chapter

- [[Gross Revenue Retention]] — retention without expansion
- [[Net Revenue Retention]] — retention including expansion/contraction (drives hypergrowth when > 1)
- [[Monthly Recurring Revenue]] — base unit for GRR/NRR calculations
- [[Lifetime Value]] — `LTV = Σ ARR(t)` over customer lifetime
- [[Annual Contract Value]] — per-deal revenue
- [[Annual Recurring Revenue]] — annualised recurring revenue

## Role in the stack

- Sits on top of the [[Data Model]] (Bowtie) and below the [[Operating Model]]
- Wrong [[Revenue Model]] or [[Data Model]] → wrong growth formula → operational plans that won't work
- The "causality" layer: analyses *why* revenue behaves as it does and predicts *what* will happen under different scenarios

## Practical applications

- **Scenario analysis** — test the impact of a discount change, a conversion-rate drop, an NRR improvement
- **Unit economics sanity check** — "To create $X in ARR, we need Y leads per month"
- **CSM vs Sales layoff decisions** — modelling LTV impact reveals a CSM cut typically costs more revenue than a Sales Manager cut, because the CSM's output compounds through NRR over years
- **Growth formula construction** — build one per GTM motion; normalize on a single win for unit-economics clarity

# Revenue Model

## Definition

The foundational layer of the [[Six-Layer Model Stack]]. Encompasses a company's [[Monetization Strategy]], [[Pricing and Packaging]], and [[Business Model]] — i.e. *how* the business earns income and the rules that govern it. Visualised as an **arc** with [[Ownership]] on the left, [[Subscription]] in the middle, [[Consumption]] on the right, and [[Freemium]] at the far edge.

## Components

1. **Monetization Strategy** — see [[Monetization Strategy]]. Three canonical forms: [[Ownership]] (pay to own), [[Subscription]] (pay to use), [[Consumption]] (pay per use). [[Freemium]] is an outlier.
2. **Pricing and Packaging** — see [[Pricing and Packaging]]. The cost customers pay and the bundles they can choose.
3. **Business Model** — see [[Business Model]]. Monetization + pricing/packaging + target market + go-to-market approach.

## Six characteristics that shift along the arc

| Characteristic | Ownership | Subscription | Consumption | Freemium |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Seller objective | Sell [[Value and Impact\|value]] | Deliver [[Value and Impact\|impact]] | Deliver impact per use | Monetize indirectly (data, upsell) |
| [[Risk Shift]] | Buyer bears risk | Balanced, shifting to seller | Seller bears risk | Seller bears all risk |
| Price (1st year) | $1,000,000s | $10,000s | $100s | $0 |
| [[Win Rate]] | 1:3 | 1:5 (annual), 1:6–7 (monthly) | 1:10 | 1:100 |
| [[Revenue Velocity]] (acquisition) | 9–18 months | 30+ days (annual), 10+ days (monthly) | 1+ day | Minutes |
| Retention (annual) | ~100% | >90% annual, >78% monthly | Lower | Lowest |

## When to use

- Auditing whether your **metrics match your monetization strategy**. Most common failure: subscription business running ownership-era pipeline math (3x pipeline when 5x is needed).
- Deciding whether to introduce a new GTM motion with a different monetization strategy. Keep it to **one monetization strategy with ≤3 GTM motions** if under $100M ARR.
- Planning a [[Phase Shift]] (e.g. perpetual → subscription, as Adobe did). Takes years; is transformational, not tactical.

## Key properties

- Position on the arc determines everything above it in the [[Six-Layer Model Stack]]. A wrong position propagates up: wrong data (Data Model) → wrong formula (Mathematical Model) → wrong operations (Operating Model) → non-sustainable growth (Growth Model) → wrong GTM motions (GTM Model).
- The arc is also a **value → impact** axis. Ownership sells the promise of value; subscription/consumption deliver impact (see [[Value and Impact]]).
- Crossing between monetization strategies is a [[Phase Shift]], not a pivot. Requires cultural, technological, and commercial redesign.

## Four common mistakes (from §5.3)

1. Applying ownership metrics to a subscription business.
2. Changing monetization strategy mid-flight without treating it as a multi-year transformation.
3. Running multiple monetization strategies simultaneously.
4. Confusing [[Recurring vs Re-occurring Revenue]].

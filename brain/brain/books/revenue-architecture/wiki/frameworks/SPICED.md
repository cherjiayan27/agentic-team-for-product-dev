# SPICED

## Definition

The standardised **customer-centric methodology** that serves as the interface layer of the [[Operating Model]]. Five elements. Forms a common language across marketing, sales, and customer success — and across multiple GTM motions. Anchored on Impact (the one constant across the customer journey).

## The five elements

| Letter | Element | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| **S** | **Situation** | The customer's existing state — what's happening today |
| **P** | **Pain** | The dysfunction in that situation; what's not working |
| **I** | **Impact** | The measurable outcome if Pain is resolved — **the anchor** |
| **C** | **Critical Event** | The deadline or event that creates urgency (distinct from Compelling) |
| **D** | **Decision** | How the choice is made — maps to the [[Buying Center]] |

## Why Impact anchors everything

- [[First Principles of Recurring Revenue]] Principle 5: Recurring Revenue is the result of [[Recurring Impact]].
- Every stage of the [[Bowtie]] centers on Impact — upstream (promise of Impact) to downstream (Impact realised).
- See [[Rational vs Emotional Impact]] for the two dimensions.

## How SPICED unifies GTM

Before SPICED: marketing talks about MQLs and SQLs, sales talks about MEDDIC criteria and BANT budget, CS talks about adoption and usage. Each function speaks its own language, which fragments the customer experience.

After SPICED: every function speaks Situation / Pain / Impact / Critical Event / Decision — using the customer's own language. Data flows coherently across the [[Bowtie]]. Handoffs are cleaner.

## Discovery process

Uncover Impact by **peeling the onion** (Figure 8.15), not by asking directly:

1. 2–3 **Situation** questions (from prior research)
2. 1–2 **Pain** questions (to find the dysfunction)
3. 3–7 **Impact** questions (to measure what solving the pain would deliver)

Then surface Critical Event: "When do you need this by?" → "What happens if you miss that date?"

## Storytelling with SPICED

Lay SPICED on top of the [[Man in the Hole]] story arc (Kurt Vonnegut):
- **Part 1** — Situation (similar customer scenario)
- **Part 2** — Pain + negative Impact of inaction (the "falling")
- **Part 3** — Positive Impact after solving it

Rocky Balboa is the canonical example.

## SPICED vs other methodologies

SPICED is **not** replacing BANT, MEDDIC, CHAMP, SPIN, etc. — it's the *interface* each of those methodologies plugs into. BANT maps onto SPICED roughly as Budget→D, Authority→D, Need→P, Timeline→CE.

Different motions use different Means/Methods while all reporting via SPICED elements.

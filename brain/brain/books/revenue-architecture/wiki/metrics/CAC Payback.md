# CAC Payback

*Partial — defined as a Sustainability-stage KPI in Ch 10; full calculation likely in Ch 10.4.*

## Definition

The time required to recover [[CAC]] from the customer's revenue. A key indicator of a motion's [[Sustainability]].

## Informal formula

```
CAC Payback (months) = CAC / (Monthly Gross Margin per Customer)
```

Or simplified when gross margin ≈ 80%:

```
CAC Payback (months) ≈ CAC / (ARR / 12 × 0.80)
```

## Benchmarks

- **<12 months** — excellent, premium valuation signal
- **12–18 months** — healthy for most B2B SaaS
- **18–24 months** — acceptable for enterprise/high-touch motions
- **>24 months** — concerning; motion may be unsustainable

## Why it matters

- Defines how quickly a GTM motion self-funds
- Pair with [[Net Revenue Retention]]: CAC Payback of 12 months + NRR of 120% means a customer is cash-positive within a year and growing — textbook [[Hypergrowth]] unit economics
- [[Rule of 40]]-style trade-off: short CAC Payback often costs growth rate

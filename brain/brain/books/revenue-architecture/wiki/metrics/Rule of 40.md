# Rule of 40

*Partial — Ch 9 references it as a Grownup-stage benchmark. Full treatment likely in Ch 9.2.12 (Breakpoint 12: Profitability).*

## Definition

A valuation heuristic applied to companies that have surpassed ~$100M ARR: **growth rate (%) + profit margin (%) ≥ 40%**.

```
Rule of 40: Growth Rate + EBITDA Margin ≥ 40%
```

## Why it matters

- As the [[Denominator Effect]] naturally slows growth at scale, pure growth rate becomes a weaker valuation signal.
- Investors start rewarding the trade-off between growth and profitability. Companies that miss the Rule of 40 are seen as zombie SaaS.
- Combines the two opposing levers Grownups can pull:
  - Continue investing for growth → lower margins, higher growth rate
  - Optimise for profit → higher margins, lower growth rate

## Example

- 50% growth + 0% profit margin = 50 (passes)
- 20% growth + 25% profit margin = 45 (passes)
- 15% growth + 10% profit margin = 25 (fails)

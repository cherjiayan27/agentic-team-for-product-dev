# Account Manager

*Stub — full treatment in Ch 8. Partial definition from Ch 6.*

## Scope

The account-owning revenue role focused on **proactive expansion** — upsells, cross-sells, geographic expansion, price increases. More akin to an acquisition-focused salesperson than a CSM. Takes a long-horizon view of customer revenue (not just next deal).

## What an AM does that a CSM doesn't

- **Proactive upsell** — reactive-only upselling (the CSM default) misses meaningful revenue
- **Cross-sell** — the most complicated expansion type (see [[Four Types of Expansion]]); requires hunting skill, sometimes displacing a competitor
- **Champion management** — mitigates Champion churn risk; cultivates multi-threaded relationships

## What an AM should NOT do

- **Cross-sell handled by a CSM** is a red flag — the CSM is incentivised on retention, not net-new, and doesn't have the sales skills for competitive displacement.
- **Renewals handled by Sales** collapses the expansion-cost ratio (see [[Retention]] risk section).

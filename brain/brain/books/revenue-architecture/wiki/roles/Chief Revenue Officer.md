# Chief Revenue Officer

*Stub — introduced briefly in Chapter 2 in the context of a specific failure pattern. Expand in later chapters.*

## Scope

Executive responsible for revenue generation across the recurring revenue business — typically encompassing sales, often extending to marketing and customer success depending on the org design.

## Incentive trap flagged in Chapter 2

Jacco calls out a specific failure pattern: newly-appointed CROs with bonuses tied to near-term revenue targets often reassign the sales (acquisition) team to handle renewals and expansions. This:
- Pushes retention cost from ~10% toward acquisition-level cost (~100%).
- Pushes expansion cost from ~20% toward acquisition-level cost.
- Causes a sharp drop in long-run profit (Figure 2.7 in the source).
- Is typically invisible in the year the decision is made because the damage accrues across the customer lifetime.

The root cause Jacco identifies is the mismatch between the CRO's short tenure / short-horizon incentives and the multi-year compounding nature of the recurring revenue engine (Principle 3 of [[First Principles of Recurring Revenue]]).

# Customer Success Manager

## Scope

The post-sale revenue role responsible for customer **retention, adoption, and renewal**. Owns the right side of the [[Bowtie]] for assigned accounts. Works in one of three [[Customer Success Motions]] approaches: Volume-Based, Vertical-Based, or Account-Based.

## Approaches

- **Volume-Based CSM** — up to 500 accounts each; efficiency-focused; relies heavily on [[Customer Success Motions|community]] for support scaling
- **Vertical-Based CSM** — specialises in one industry; deeper domain expertise; for [[Medium Touch GTM Motion]] / [[High Touch GTM Motion]]
- **Account-Based CSM** — one large F500 account; dedicated; for [[Dedicated Touch GTM Motion]]

## What a CSM does

- Onboarding and time-to-first-impact
- Adoption and engagement tracking
- Renewals and churn prevention
- Reactive upsell (not proactive — that's the [[Account Manager]])
- Customer advocacy programs
- Feedback and insights gathering
- Health scoring
- Customer education

## What a CSM should NOT do

- **Cross-sell** — that belongs to the AM (see [[Four Types of Expansion]]); CSMs lack the hunting skill and the right compensation structure
- **New-logo acquisition** — entirely different sales motion

## Common mistake

Reassigning CSMs to handle expansion cross-sells or renewals-as-sales. Pushes costs toward acquisition ratios and damages [[Net Revenue Retention]] (see [[Retention]] risk section).

# Decider

## Definition

The member of a [[Buying Center]] who makes the final purchasing decision. Has a deadline and a deliverable — unlike a [[Champion]], who has neither. Maps to SPICED elements Impact (I) and Critical Event (CE).

## Decider vs Champion

The distinction is precise:

- **[[Champion]]** sees the solution in action (e.g. a demo) and believes. No authority. No deadline. No deliverable.
- **Decider** has the authority to allocate resources to achieve a specific Impact within a specific timeframe. Authority. Deadline. Deliverable.

## Why sellers must address the Decider differently

From §8.2.1 Use Case:

- Decider responds to **rational Impact** (revenue, cost, time savings)
- Champion responds to **emotional Impact** (user experience, job risk, promotion)
- Seller's dual job: (1) uncover the Decider's rational Impact through the Champion's emotional lens; (2) equip the Champion to sell the rational Impact upward internally

## The four-moment sequence (§8.2.1)

1. **Discovery call** — seller empathizes with Champion's emotional Pain; uncovers Decider's rational Impact via Champion ("What's important to your CRO?")
2. **Follow-up** — seller re-frames back with quantified Impact ("We can help your CRO add $200K in 90 days")
3. **Alignment call** — Champion confirms Decider's desired Impact; seller provides supporting data
4. **On-site stakeholder meeting** — seller demonstrates how the product achieves the Decider's Impact by the Critical Event date

## The Critical Event trap

The Decider's Critical Event (fear of being fired, missing a growth target) is often **just a Compelling Event for the stakeholder team**. What's urgent at the top is merely interesting further down. Sellers must read this accurately or lose deals.

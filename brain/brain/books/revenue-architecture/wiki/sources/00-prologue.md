# Prologue — The Golden Gate Bridge Metaphor

**Source:** `raw/markdown/00-prologue.md` (book pages 8–9)

## Thesis

The Golden Gate Bridge — nearly a century old, built on scientific principles, modular components, meticulous planning — is the metaphor for how Revenue Architecture should approach recurring revenue. Bridges last 100 years because engineers **based strategies on evidence, data, and practical experience**. Revenue Architecture applies the same scientific approach to recurring revenue.

## Key claims

- Revenue Architecture is a **scientific discipline** — evidence, data, practical experience — not gut feel
- Bridges are built from **modular components assembled to span a gap**; revenue factories are built from **modular GTM motions assembled to produce revenue**
- The GTM team pursues three factory goals: driving revenue growth, optimizing costs, improving product quality
- Two missing elements historically: an **[[Operating Model]]** to run the factory, and a **detailed growth design**
- We're at a crossroads driven by AI ([[Four Industrial Revolutions|Factory 4.0]]); the 2012–2022 decade's strategies won't carry forward
- The book is a "work in progress" — the reader plays an essential role in shaping the discipline

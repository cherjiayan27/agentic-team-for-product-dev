# Chapter 04 — Systems and Processes

**Source:** `raw/markdown/04-systems-and-processes.md` (book pages 88–120)

## Thesis

A Recurring Revenue Factory runs on **systems and processes** — the third of Part I's three fundamentals (after First Principles and Models). The classic marketing-and-sales funnel is a *revenue acquisition system* designed for perpetual revenue; it fails for recurring revenue because 2 of the 3 growth components (retention and expansion) happen outside its purview. The fix: replace the funnel with the **Bowtie** — the custom-built recurring revenue system. Organisations must also shift from **people-centric** (hire more, fire fast, opinion-based) to **process-centric** (data-centric, causality-based) culture.

## Key claims

- **The funnel is a revenue acquisition system with three sub-systems** (Figure 4.1):
  1. **Awareness** — prospects → leads
  2. **Education** — leads → opportunities
  3. **Selection** — opportunities → closed deals
  4. The three combined are the fourth sub-system: the acquisition system itself
- **Five constraints of the classic funnel** (§4.1.2):
  1. Doesn't cover recurring revenue — 2/3 of growth happens outside it
  2. Seller-centric — focuses on closing deals, not delivering Impact
  3. One-directional — assumes linear flow; real customers zigzag
  4. Operators treat it linearly — "double leads for double revenue" — ignoring the [[Nonlinearity]]
  5. Doesn't consider closed loops — no feedback mechanism
- **The [[Bowtie]] replaces the funnel as an 8-sub-system model** (Figure 4.3):
  - Left side (Acquisition): 1. Awareness → 2. Education → 3. Selection → 4. **Mutual Commit** (note: the commit is a mutual relationship start, not a sale endpoint)
  - Right side (Retention & Expansion): 5. **Onboarding** (Activation in PLG) → 6. **Adoption** → 7. **Expansion**
  - Sub-System 8: the **GTM System** — all 7 together as a coherent whole
- **The orchestra analogy.** Each section (percussion, brass, strings) is a sub-system. The orchestra as a whole has unique characteristics. If one section falls out of sync the whole performance suffers — regardless of each musician's skill. The conductor ensures cohesion. **Who is the GTM system's conductor?** Today it's the CEO by default. Jacco's bet: eventually a role with financial-modelling background.
- **System vs sub-system distinction is critical.** Optimising each sub-system independently doesn't produce a coherent GTM system. Cross-functional orchestration is the hidden skill.
- **People-centric vs process-centric culture.** People-centric = hire more when things go well, fire fast when they don't; opinion-based. Process-centric = scrutinise the process, data first; causality-based. The distinction drives long-term outcomes far more than individual-hire quality.
- **Three revenue-factory goals mapped to systems and processes:**
  - Achieve Growth → closed-loop systems
  - Lower Costs → iterative process improvement (technology, ordinary people, or both)
  - Recurring Impact → emphasis on what the product *delivers*, not what it *is*
- **Customer centricity defined:** not just "customer first" — it's specifically "ensure the customer achieves the desired Impact from the product." Every Bowtie stage viewed from the customer's perspective.
- **Adoption → Retention → Expansion** — each stage has its own process. Adoption = first use; Retention = consistent use over time; Expansion = broadening use (more seats, more products, more regions).

# Chapter 09 — The Growth Model

**Source:** `raw/markdown/09-growth-model.md` (original PDF: `raw/pdf-chapters/09-growth-model.pdf`, book pages 306–378)

## Thesis

Recurring revenue businesses follow a **similar, predictable trajectory** — the S-curve — and encounter the **same 12 revenue breakpoints** on the way. Growth is not a metric to drive valuation; it's an operational tool that forecasts resource needs 12–18 months in advance. Most companies fail not from bad products but from failing to navigate the breakpoints (wrong monetization, wrong GTM motion, unrepeatable processes, unsustainable costs, productivity collapse).

## Key claims

- **Context:** As of late 2021, <2% of funded cloud companies reach $100M ARR; <0.2% reach $1B ARR. By 2023 the ratio of A-round → IPO deteriorated from 1:45 to 1:60.
- **Growth follows an S-curve** (Figure 9.2, DataDog example) — slow start, middle-stage compounding via [[Recurring Revenue]], eventual plateau at TAM saturation. See [[S-Curve]].
- **Growth patterns** (§9.1.1): Linear, Exponential/J-curve, Logarithmic, S-curve (logistic), Bell Curve. Subscription businesses align with S-curve due to [[Compound Impact]].
- **Growth rate math** — three flavors:
  - **Annual Growth Rate** = (End − Begin) / Begin × 100%
  - **CAGR** (Compound Annual Growth Rate) = (End/Begin)^(1/years) − 1
  - **CMGR** (Compound Monthly Growth Rate) = (End/Begin)^(1/12) − 1
  - CMGR >10% = healthy for Series A/B (roughly 3x YoY)
- **Growth rate labels** (Table 9.2):
  - Normal 0–10% / CMGR 0.8%
  - Rapid 10–20% / CMGR 1.5%
  - Hypergrowth 20–40% / CMGR 2.7%
  - Double 100% / CMGR 5.9%
  - Triple 200% / CMGR 9.9%
  - **Blitzscaling >1000%** / CMGR >21% — Shopify, OpenAI, Uber, Airbnb
- **[[Denominator Effect]]** — as base revenue grows, the same absolute ARR add represents a smaller %. Not a sign of deterioration; mathematically unavoidable.
- **Growth Stages** (Table 9.3):
  - Seed (angel, up to $1M, 1–2 yrs) — achieve $1M
  - Startup (A/B, up to $10M, 2–4 yrs) — 100–200% growth
  - Scaleup (C/D/E, up to $300M, 3–6 yrs) — 60–100%
  - Grownup (IPO+, beyond $300M, 2–4 yrs) — 10–60%
  - Enterprise (public, beyond $10B, 20+ yrs) — <10%
- **[[T2D3 Growth Trend]]** (Neeraj Agrawal, Battery Ventures, 2015) — after $1M ARR: triple, triple, double, double, double → ~$100M over 5 years.
- **[[Inflection Point]]** — where growth direction shifts. Pivotal decision: keep investing for market expansion, or pivot to profitability.
- **Growth Funding Stages funnel (Table 9.4):** from $2M seed, ~45% reach PMF (A-round), ~27% reach GTMF (B-round), ~14% become Scaleups (C-round), ~6% reach $100M ARR (D-round), ~1% IPO.
- **Breakpoints are not one-time events.** They apply per GTM motion and must be re-navigated when launching new motions, new segments, new regions, new products.

3. Data Model and Structure
4. GTM Model selection

7. Velocity (Scalability)
8. Sustainability (Cost of Growth)
9. Productivity
10. Interoperability
11. Durability

# Chapter 11 — Simply Deploy

**Source:** `raw/markdown/11-simply-deploy.md` (book pages 449–477) — Part IV intro (pp. 449–451) + Ch 11 proper

## Thesis

Every prior chapter was about understanding. Ch 11 is about **deploying** Revenue Architecture in a company with $10M–$10B ARR. The deployment philosophy: **Think Big, Act Small, Move Fast**. Build ambitious factory vision (Think Big), apply it through Moments That Matter (Act Small), and iterate via short sprints (Move Fast). Simplicity on the customer-facing surface is expensive — it requires deep engineering underneath.

## Key claims

- **Watchmaker analogy.** Billions glance at watches without thinking about the gears. The simplicity is a deliberate choice that conceals complexity. Revenue Architecture is the same: a simple customer experience requires a carefully engineered revenue propulsion engine underneath.
- **Three-part deployment strategy:**
  - **Think Big** (§11.1) — Industrial-scale impact via the 4 core models: Growth Model + GTM Model + Data Model + Operating Model
  - **Act Small** (§11.2) — [[Moments That Matter]] (MTMs); small, high-leverage interactions
  - **Move Fast** (§11.3) — short sprints; find what works; do more of it; stop what doesn't
- **GTM team has three layers:**
  - **Executives** — visionaries, strategists
  - **Managers** — enforcers, optimizers
  - **Individual Performers** — doers, deliverers
- **Limit the executive team to ~10** for agility. CEO leads. Functional heads (marketing, sales, CS) + Head of Product + CFO/FP&A.
- **Steward model.** Assign one executive per chapter/model as the steward. They become the primary point of contact for everything related to that model. Becomes the implementation scaffolding.
- **Model sequencing for implementation** (Figure 11.1):
  - Growth Model first — establish where you are on the S-curve
  - GTM Model second — structure motions aligned with the growth plan
  - Data Model third — transition each motion from funnel to Bowtie
  - Operating Model fourth — blueprint MTMs to deliver optimal experience at key moments
- **[[Moments That Matter]]** — sellers guide buyers through hundreds of actions over customer lifetime, but only ~**a dozen actions deeply impact the customer**. These MTMs are where disproportionate improvement is possible. Quick response time during downtime is a canonical example.
- **Marginal-gains compounding via MTMs.** Not "boil the ocean" transformation. Identify 10–12 MTMs, blueprint each, measure, iterate. The [[Compound Impact]] across MTMs produces system-wide hypergrowth.
- **Part IV / Chapter 11 is a *playbook*, not theory.** Contains Actions (11.1 through the end) — specific exercises for the executive team: brief the team, assign stewards, identify the growth plan, map revenue breakpoints achieved/missed/in-progress, structure GTM motions, establish Growth Formulas per motion.

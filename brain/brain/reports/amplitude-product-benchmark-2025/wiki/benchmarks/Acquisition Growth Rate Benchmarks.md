# Acquisition Growth Rate Benchmarks

## Definition

Monthly new-user growth rate benchmarks from Amplitude's 2025 Product Benchmark Report. **Power-law distributed** — top 10% grow ~9× faster than the median.

## Data (Figures 1, 2, 4)

### All products

| Percentile | Monthly growth rate | Approximate annual |
|---|---|---|
| 50th (median) | 0.11% | ~1.3% |
| 75th | (mid-range, not explicitly stated) | — |
| 90th (top 10%) | 8.7% | ~174% |

### Enterprise

| Percentile | Monthly growth rate |
|---|---|
| 50th (median) | (low single digits) |
| 75th | ~3% |
| 90th (top 10%) | 8.8% |

### Concentration

Top 10% of products account for **>80% of all new users added**. The remaining 90% of products compete for **<20% of new-user volume**.

## How to interpret

- **If your monthly growth is ~0.1%**: you're at median. Not failing, but not breaking out. Compounding effect is minimal.
- **If monthly growth is 1–5%**: above average. Meaningful compounding over 12 months.
- **If monthly growth is 8%+**: top-decile. Category-defining growth. Requires strong activation + retention to sustain.

## Critical caveat: vanity metric risk

Amplitude's data (Figure 3) shows **no correlation between top-quartile acquisition and top-quartile retention**. High growth on this metric does NOT predict business outcomes. See [[Leaky Bucket Problem]].

Pair this benchmark with [[Retention Benchmarks (All Products)]] always. Both in top quartile → genuine top performer. Only acquisition in top quartile → leaky bucket risk.

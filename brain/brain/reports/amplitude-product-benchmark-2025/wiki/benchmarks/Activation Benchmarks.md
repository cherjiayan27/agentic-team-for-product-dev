# Activation Benchmarks

## Definition

Cross-industry [[Activation Rate]] benchmarks from Amplitude's 2025 Product Benchmark Report. Activation is the earliest meaningful signal of long-term retention potential.

## Data (Figures 5, 7)

### All products

| Period | 50th percentile (median) | 90th percentile (top 10%) |
|---|---|---|
| Day 1 | 5% | **21%** (4× gap) |
| Day 14 | 2% | **9%** (4.5× gap) |

**For half of all products, >98% of new users are inactive two weeks after their first action.**

### Enterprise

| Period | Median (50th) | Top 10% (90th) |
|---|---|---|
| Day 7 | 2.1% | **12.4%** (**6× gap**) |

### The "7 at 7" rule

**7% of cohort returning on Day 7** puts a product in the top 25% for activation. Consistently predicts strong 3-month retention.

## Interpretation tiers

| Day-7 activation | Tier | Action |
|---|---|---|
| < 2% | Bottom quartile | Fix onboarding immediately; retention is likely broken |
| 2–4% | Median | Room for improvement; focus on first-week experience |
| 4–7% | Upper median | Optimise the aha moment |
| 7%+ | Top 25% | Strong signal of future retention |
| 10%+ (enterprise) | Top 10% enterprise | Category-leading |

## Why activation is a better leading indicator than acquisition

Amplitude's data (Figure 3 vs Figure 6):
- Acquisition quartile vs retention quartile → **no correlation**
- Activation quartile vs retention quartile → **strong correlation**

Action implication: **invest in activation before acquisition**. Top-of-funnel spend without strong activation is the [[Leaky Bucket Problem]].

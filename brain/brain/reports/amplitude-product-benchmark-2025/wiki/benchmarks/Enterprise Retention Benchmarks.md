# Enterprise Retention Benchmarks

## Definition

Enterprise-segment [[User Retention]] benchmarks from Amplitude's 2025 Product Benchmark Report. Tighter spread than the all-products view in [[Retention Benchmarks (All Products)]] — and the top/median gap is **even wider** (7× vs 5×), which amounts to millions in recurring revenue at enterprise scale.

## Data (Figure 13)

At Month 3:

| Percentile | Enterprise retention rate |
|---|---|
| Median (50th) | 3.5% |
| Top 10% (90th) | 25% |

**~7× gap** between median and top 10%.

## Financial significance

At enterprise scale, every percentage point of retention translates directly to recurring revenue. Amplitude's framing:

> "For enterprises, the ability to keep users around is where the real challenge and opportunity lies... at the enterprise level, it translates to millions in recurring revenue and long-term customer value. For enterprises, retention is a growth multiplier on every dollar spent acquiring and serving customers."

Ties directly to:
- [[Net Revenue Retention]] — user retention is a leading indicator for NRR
- [[Lifetime Value]] — LTV scales with retention
- [[Hypergrowth]] — only NRR > 1 produces hypergrowth, and that requires strong user retention

## Why the gap is worse in enterprise

Possible explanations (inference, not directly stated in the report):
- Enterprise products often have multi-stakeholder usage — need ALL stakeholder types to retain, not just the primary user
- Enterprise purchase cycles attract tire-kickers (POCs, competitive evaluations) who inflate initial user counts without ever retaining
- Enterprise products face more "seat inflation" — contracts include seats that never activate

*Inference:* if the goal is 90th percentile enterprise retention (25%), invest specifically in **multi-persona activation** and **stakeholder-wide Champion development** (see [[Champion]]). Single-user activation won't cut it.

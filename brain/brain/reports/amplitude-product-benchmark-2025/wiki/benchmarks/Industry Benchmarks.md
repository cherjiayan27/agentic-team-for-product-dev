# Industry Benchmarks

## Definition

Per-industry cuts of Amplitude's four core metrics (Acquisition, Activation, Engagement, Retention) from the 2025 Product Benchmark Report. Use these instead of [[Retention Benchmarks (All Products)]], [[Engagement Benchmarks]], etc. when you know your vertical — they're more precise.

## Six industries

### Summary: Monthly Acquisition + 3-Month Retention

| Industry | Monthly Acquisition (median / 90th) | 3-Month Retention (median / 90th) |
|---|---|---|
| **B2B technology** | 0.3% / 9% | 2.5% / 15.6% |
| **Ecommerce** | 0.03% / 7.5% | 2.8% / 18.9% |
| **Financial services** | 0.5% / 10.2% | 4.6% / 19.5% |
| **Healthcare** | 0.7% / 7.3% | 2.1% / 10.6% |
| **Media & entertainment** | -0.2% / 8% | 2.5% / 13.4% |

## Key observations

- **Travel & hospitality dominates both ranks** — post-COVID rebound + strong inherent usage loops
- **Financial services and ecommerce cluster** — both benefit from frequent transactional usage
- **B2B tech is near-bottom on retention** despite strong acquisition (9% top-decile) — consistent with enterprise complexity slowing retention onboarding
- **Healthcare is bottom-ranked** — low natural usage frequency + regulatory friction
- **Media & entertainment has negative median acquisition (-0.2%)** — industry-wide user loss at the median company

## How to use

- **Find your industry row.** If you're below median, prioritise structural fixes.
- **Use the 90th-percentile number as a realistic stretch goal**, not an aspirational dream.
- **Cross-reference industry retention with your [[Product Usage Interval]].** Healthcare's 2.1% median makes sense for an industry with quarterly-to-annual usage; B2B's 2.5% is concerning given daily expected use.

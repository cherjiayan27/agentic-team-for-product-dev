# Retention Benchmarks (All Products)

## Definition

Cross-industry [[User Retention]] benchmarks from Amplitude's 2025 Product Benchmark Report, aggregated across their full customer base. **Use as a sanity check, not a target** — industry-specific benchmarks ([[Enterprise Retention Benchmarks]] and the per-industry snapshots) are more precise.

## Data (Figure 12)

| Period | 50th percentile (median) | 75th percentile | 90th percentile (top 10%) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Month 1 | 6.5% | — | 26.0% |
| Month 2 | — | — | — |
| Month 3 | 3.8% | — | 18.5% |

**Key takeaways:**
- Top products retain users at **~4× the median rate** in month 1
- **The gap widens over time.** By month 3, top 10% retain 5× better
- **96% of the median product's users churn by the end of month 3**

(Percentile values for 75th not reported in the chapter text; likely visible in the figure chart but not extracted here.)

## How to use this benchmark

- **If you're below the 50th percentile** — product-market-fit problem, not a marketing problem. More users won't help; they'll churn faster.
- **If you're between 50th and 90th** — optimise activation and early-week experience. Most leverage here.
- **If you're at 90th+** — optimise for engagement depth and expansion rather than retention. See [[Durability]].

## Methodology note

Amplitude's data comes from their customer base (primarily product-led / data-instrumented companies). **Bias:** skews toward product-aware organisations that actively measure cohort retention. Median may be higher than the universe of all SaaS products (including undermeasured ones).

# Product Usage Interval

## Definition

The **natural frequency at which users engage with a product**. A messaging app's interval is hourly; a real-estate platform's is quarterly. Understanding this interval is the prerequisite for designing effective engagement and retention strategies.

## Why it matters

- **Measuring engagement against the wrong interval misleads.** A product with a quarterly natural usage rhythm will look "unengaged" by daily-active-user metrics.
- **Triggers, notifications, and re-engagement must match the interval.** A weekly email for an app used hourly is spam; the same email for an app used quarterly is a service.
- **Retention benchmarks must be interpreted against the interval.** Month-1 retention of 6% is bad for a daily-use app but may be structurally normal for a quarterly-use one.

## Identifying your product's interval

Ask: *when users are getting value, how often does that value need to be delivered?*

- **Hourly / daily**: messaging, social, productivity, work tools
- **Weekly**: entertainment, social media check-ins, fitness
- **Monthly**: banking, subscription management, analytics dashboards
- **Quarterly / seasonal**: real estate, travel, tax prep, compliance
- **Annual**: health insurance renewal, tax filing, performance reviews

## Design implication

> "Even products with naturally lower usage, like real estate platforms, can think about content and features to increase engagement between major transactions, such as a home value check." — Amplitude Ch 4

For low-natural-interval products, the design challenge is **filling in the gaps** with low-friction value (content, updates, useful notifications) to maintain habit formation without forcing unnatural usage.

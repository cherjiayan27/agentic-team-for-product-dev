# How Top Products Grow (Amplitude Benchmark Ch 1)

**Source:** `raw/markdown/01-how-top-products-grow.md` (Amplitude Product Benchmark Report 2025, PDF pp. 3–5)

## Thesis

Top 10% of products systematically outperform the rest across all four key metrics (Acquisition, Activation, Engagement, Retention). The secret is **winning the first week** — 69% of top week-1 activators are also top 3-month retainers. Meanwhile, the "leaky bucket" is real: top-quartile acquisition does NOT correlate with top-quartile retention. Pouring water into a bucket with holes doesn't fill it up.

## Key findings

- **It pays to be the best.** Top 10% products outperform on every metric. Enterprise-segment pattern mirrors the all-product pattern.
- **First impressions matter.** Week-1 activation is the single strongest predictor of 3-month retention. **69% of top week-1 activators are also top 3-month retainers.** Direct empirical support for Jacco's [[Recurring Impact]] principle.
- **[[Leaky Bucket Problem]] is real.** No correlation between top-quartile acquisition and top-quartile retention. Fixing the funnel starts with retention, not acquisition.
- **Travel and hospitality lead across industries.** Followed by financial services and ecommerce. (Details in [[06-industry-snapshot]].)
- **Enterprises** defined as 2,000+ Americas employees or 1,500+ international. Studied separately across all metrics.

## Four metric definitions

Critical for reading the rest of the report:

| Metric | Definition | Source page |
|---|---|---|
| **Acquisition** | % change in new users over a period | [[02-acquisition]] + [[Acquisition]] (Jacco) |
| **Activation** | % of users who return shortly after their first visit | [[Activation Rate]] — introduced in [[03-activation]] |
| **Engagement** | % change in active users over a period | [[03-engagement]] |
| **Retention** | % of users who return in a given month after first visit | [[User Retention]] (distinct from Jacco's [[Retention]]) |

All benchmarks based on **data from over a one-year period**. Presented at the 50th / 75th / 90th percentiles for all products, plus enterprise-specific.

# Acquisition (Amplitude Benchmark Ch 2)

**Source:** `raw/markdown/02-acquisition.md` (Amplitude Product Benchmark Report 2025, PDF pp. 6–10)

## Thesis

The acquisition growth gap is enormous — **top 10% products grow ~9× faster than the median**. Top 10% account for **>80% of all new users added**. But new user growth is a vanity metric without retention. Amplitude's data shows **no correlation between acquisition and retention** quartiles — confirming the [[Leaky Bucket Problem]]. Fast-growing products look healthy on the top-of-funnel metric and may be structurally broken underneath.

## Key benchmarks (Figures 1, 2, 4)

| Segment | Monthly growth rate | Annualised |
|---|---|---|
| **Median (50th)** | 0.11% | ~1.3% |
| **Top 10% (90th)** | 8.7% | **~174%** |
| **Enterprise top 10%** | 8.8% | **~175%** |
| **Enterprise 75th** | ~3% | ~43% |

**Compounding effect:** if three companies each add 100 new users in January:
- 90th percentile ends the year with **274 new users**
- Median: **103** (barely moves)

**Concentration:** top 10% capture **>80% of all new users**. The acquisition market is power-law distributed.

## Key findings

- **No correlation between acquisition and retention quartiles** (Figure 3). Top-quartile acquirers distribute evenly across retention quartiles. Vanity metric confirmation.
- **Acquisition ↔ engagement correlation exists** in the short term. Fast growth brings activity — but activity ≠ loyalty.
- **Enterprises benefit from brand + budget advantages.** Even lower-percentile enterprises can use this to accelerate acquisition if they think full-funnel.

## Tactics (5-step acquisition rebuild)

1. **Track more than signups** — behavioural segmentation to identify which users become long-term customers
2. **Refine channels with behavioural insights** — top performers concentrate on a few channels, don't spread
3. **Use retention patterns to power acquisition** — lookalike audiences from your best-retaining users
4. **Build a growth engine focused on quality** — product + marketing collaboration, shared growth goals, experimentation culture
5. **Reference:** Amplitude's Mastering Customer Acquisition Strategy Playbook

# Activation (Amplitude Benchmark Ch 3)

**Source:** `raw/markdown/03-activation.md` (Amplitude Product Benchmark Report 2025, PDF pp. 11–15)

## Thesis

Activation is the first real sign that a user has experienced enough value to come back — the "[[Aha Moment]]." Unlike acquisition, activation **does** predict retention: **69% of top 7-day activators are also top 3-month retainers**. The magic threshold: **7% of original cohort returning on day 7 puts a product in the top 25%** for activation. For the median product, **>98% of new users are inactive two weeks after their first action**.

### The magic number: 7% on Day 7

Hitting **7% of cohort returning on day 7** lands a product in the top 25% for activation — which consistently correlates with strong 3-month retention.

## Key findings

- **Activation ↔ retention correlation is strong** (Figure 6) — unlike the non-correlated acquisition ↔ retention relationship
- **69% of top 7-day activators are also top 3-month retainers**
- **The first few days are the critical window** for showing value
- Enterprise upside is huge: 6× gap between top 10% and median means enterprise onboarding investment has outsized returns

## Tactics (from "Take action" — speed up time to value)

1. **Define your [[North Star Metric]]** — not "returning to the app" but the action that matches your value proposition ("booking a trip", not "opening the app")
2. **Identify where users drop off** — session replay to find friction points
3. **Keep users motivated** — gamification, progress bars, celebration moments
4. **Get to know your users** — progressive profiling during onboarding (role, use case) to personalise early experience
5. **Reverse-engineer your best users** — cohort analysis to find what loyal users do in their first week; encourage those behaviours in new users
6. **Leverage social proof** — similar-use-case customer stories

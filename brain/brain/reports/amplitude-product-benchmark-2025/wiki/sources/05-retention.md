# Retention (Amplitude Benchmark Ch 5)

**Source:** `raw/markdown/05-retention.md` (Amplitude Product Benchmark Report 2025, PDF pp. 21–24)

## Thesis

**Retention is a product's ability to keep users coming back**, measured as the percentage of users who return in a given month after their first visit. Closely tied to the product's value — and therefore the single most important metric for sustainable growth. Top-performing products retain users at **4× to 7× the median rate**. The gap widens over time: early retention gaps compound into much larger differences by month 3.

**Operational implication (echoes [[Recurring Impact]]):** the first week determines retention. Win the first week = win retention.

## Key claims (with benchmark data)

- **Industry-wide retention rates by percentile:**
  - **Month 1:** top 10% = 26%, median = 6.5% (**4× gap**)
  - **Month 2:** gap widens
  - **Month 3:** top 10% = 18.5%, median = 3.8% (**~5× gap**)
  - **By end of month 3, 96% of the median product's new users have churned.**
- **Enterprise retention is even more polarised:**
  - At month 3: top 10% enterprise = 25%, median enterprise = 3.5% (**~7× gap**)
  - At enterprise scale, 7× retention translates to millions in recurring revenue and LTV.
- **Early activation → retention linkage.** Amplitude's data shows activation speed in the first week directly predicts retention. This aligns with Jacco's Onboarding stage in the [[Bowtie]] and the importance of time-to-first-impact.
- **Retention = growth multiplier.** A McKinsey reference (cited in Ch 1 of the report): *the most successful growth companies earn 80% of their value from existing customers.* Retention compounds; acquisition doesn't.

## Tactics (from "Take action")

1. **Spot where users drop off** — lifecycle cohort analysis to find stage-to-stage conversion bottlenecks (maps to [[Bowtie Metrics]])
2. **Win back churned users** — cohort-target dormant users; often higher ROI than new acquisition
3. **Test over and over** — experimentation to de-risk optimisations
4. **Use push notifications** — Calm's 3× retention lift from daily-reminder prompt
5. **Build retention skills** — Amplitude's Mastering Customer Retention Playbook + Retention Workbook

# OKR Cascade Framework

**When relevant:** Quarterly planning. Aligning team objectives to company strategy. Ensuring product work ladders up to business goals.

---

## OKR Formula

```
Objective: [Inspirational goal statement]
├── KR1: [Metric] from [current] to [target] by [date]
├── KR2: [Metric] from [current] to [target] by [date]
└── KR3: [Metric] from [current] to [target] by [date]
```

## Example

```
Objective: Become the go-to solution for enterprise customers

KR1: Increase enterprise ARR from $5M to $8M
KR2: Improve enterprise NPS from 35 to 50
KR3: Reduce enterprise onboarding time from 30 days to 14 days
```

## The Cascade

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  COMPANY LEVEL                          │
│  Strategic objectives (CEO, Exec Team)  │
└───────────────┬─────────────────────────┘
                │
                ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  PRODUCT LEVEL                          │
│  How product contributes (CPO, Head PM) │
└───────────────┬─────────────────────────┘
                │
                ▼
┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  TEAM LEVEL                             │
│  Specific initiatives (PMs, Tech Leads) │
└─────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Alignment Scoring

| Dimension | Target | Red Flag |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| Vertical alignment | >90% | <70% |
| Horizontal alignment | >75% | <50% |
| Coverage | >80% | <60% |
| Balance | >80% | <60% |

## Writing Objectives — The 3 Cs

| Criterion | Good | Bad |
|-----------|------|-----|
| **Clear** | "Improve customer onboarding" | "Make things better" |
| **Compelling** | "Delight enterprise customers" | "Serve enterprise" |
| **Challenging** | Stretches capabilities | Easy to hit 100% |

## Key Result Scoring

| Score | Status | Description |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| 0.0-0.3 | Red | Significant gap |
| 0.4-0.6 | Yellow | Partial progress |
| 0.7-0.9 | Green | On track (0.7 = success for stretch goals) |
| 1.0 | Complete | Target achieved |

## Common Pitfalls

| Pitfall | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Too many OKRs (10+ objectives) | Limit to 3-5 per level |
| Always hitting 100% | Set stretch targets (0.7 = success) |
| KRs are tasks, not outcomes | Focus on measurable impact |
| Set and forget | Check-ins every 2 weeks |
| Team OKRs don't link up | Validate parent relationships |

## Quarterly Cadence

| Week | Activity |
|------|----------|
| Week -2 | Leadership sets company OKRs draft |
| Week -1 | Product and team OKR drafting |
| Week 0 | Finalization and alignment review |
| Week 6 | Mid-quarter review |
| Week 12 | Quarter close, scoring, learnings |
